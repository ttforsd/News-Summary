## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Mystery Donor Funds US Military Pay During Shutdown**
    *   An anonymous donor provided $130 million to cover US troops' salaries.
    *   President Trump identified the donor as a "big supporter" and a U.S. citizen.
    *   The donation raises ethics questions about military funding during government shutdowns.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c891p1pez42o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c891p1pez42o))

*   **US Inflation Hits 3%, Beef Prices Soar**
    *   US inflation reached 3% in September, its highest since January, but lower than forecasted.
    *   Beef prices jumped 12.9% for mince and 16.6% for steaks in the last year.
    *   President Trump urged ranchers to lower prices, proposing increased beef imports.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgkzgrmvdrno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgkzgrmvdrno), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr7m2pd3810o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr7m2pd3810o))

*   **UK Retail Sales Show Unexpected Strength**
    *   Retail sales volumes rose 0.5% in September, defying expectations of a decrease.
    *   Growth was driven by online gold purchases and new tech gadgets like the iPhone 17.
    *   Sales volumes were 0.9% higher over the July-September quarter compared to the previous three months.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdxrnz35k1no](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdxrnz35k1no))

*   **UK Chancellor Considers Income Tax Hike**
    *   Rachel Reeves refused to rule out an income tax rise in the upcoming November Budget.
    *   The government faces a £22bn budget shortfall, pressuring for tax increases or spending cuts.
    *   A Treasury Select Committee report advised against cutting cash ISA allowances, deeming it ineffective for investment.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgr4g89g1x8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgr4g89g1x8o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yp57peqw8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yp57peqw8o))

*   **Target Corporation Announces Significant Job Cuts**
    *   The US retail giant will cut 1,800 corporate jobs, roughly 8% of its global corporate workforce.
    *   The layoffs are part of a strategy to reverse four years of stagnant sales and address "too many layers" slowing decisions.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8g2rxpwd7o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8g2rxpwd7o))

*   **JLR Cyber-Attack Impacts UK Car Production**
    *   A five-week shutdown following a cyber-attack caused JLR production to halt in September.

    *   The incident is estimated to cost £1.9bn, making it the most damaging cyber event in UK history.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgmp1prnv0o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgmp1prnv0o))

    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy4p8k074yzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy4p8k074yzo))

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Life-Changing Eye Implant Helps Blind Patients Read**
    *   A microchip implant inserted into the retina has allowed five blind patients to read again.
    *   The international trial, led by Moorfields Eye Hospital, shows "astounding" results for dry AMD patients.
    *   Patients use special glasses with a camera to send images to the implant, then to the brain.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0qpz39jpj7o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0qpz39jpj7o))

*   **Antidepressant Side Effects Ranked for First Time**
    *   New research reveals significant differences in physical side effects among 30 common antidepressants.
    *   Impacts include weight changes (up to 2.4kg difference) and heart rate variations (up to 21 bpm).
    *   Academics call for personalized matching of drugs to patient needs and other health conditions.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9d65nqgd5zo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9d65nqgd5zo))

*   **NHS to Include Menopause Screening in Health Checks**
    *   From next year, women aged 40-74 will be asked about menopause symptoms during routine NHS health checks.
    *   The initiative aims to identify women needing advice and support, including HRT or counselling.
    *   Campaigners welcome the move to reduce stigma but raise concerns about low uptake in some communities.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq50z185d78o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq50z185d78o))

*   **UK Community Health Services Under Strain**
    *   The Care Quality Commission warns government's plan for neighbourhood health hubs is at risk.
    *   Long waiting times and stretched staffing in mental health, GP care, and social care are cited.
    *   This could lead to vulnerable patients suffering due to increased demands on inadequate services.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwyp0ngvr3vo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwyp0ngvr3vo))

*   **Resident Doctors Announce Five-Day Strike in November**
    *   The British Medical Association called for a fresh five-day strike in England after pay talks broke down.
    *   The walkout, starting November 14, is expected to cause significant disruption in hospitals.
    *   Doctors argue pay is still 20% lower than in 2008, adjusted for inflation, despite recent rises.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyk5zr7p71o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyk5zr7p71o))

*   **Tech Bosses Urged to Combat Mobile Phone Theft**
    *   MPs criticized Apple, Samsung, and Google for not doing enough to deter mobile phone thefts.
    *   The committee called for technical measures, such as blocking stolen phones overseas from cloud services.
    *   Police estimate 80,000 smartphones were stolen in London in 2024, with 78% connected to overseas networks.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2y037pg41o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2y037pg41o))

*   **No Digital ID Checks Until Job Change, Says UK No 10**
    *   The UK government confirmed its digital ID scheme will not be applied retrospectively for current workers.
    *   It will be mandatory only for new jobs entered into after the scheme's proposed launch by 2028.
    *   PM Starmer stressed it would not be compulsory for essential services like healthcare, denying surveillance concerns.
