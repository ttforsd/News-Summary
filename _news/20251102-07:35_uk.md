    *   Category 5 Hurricane Melissa made landfall in Jamaica, killing at least 19 people there and 30 in Haiti, causing widespread destruction and flooding.

    *   Impact: Major humanitarian crisis with urgent needs for shelter, clean water, food, and fuel; complex aid distribution due to blocked roads.

    *   Monsoon floods have killed over 1,000 people and affected 6.9 million in Pakistan, driven by melting glaciers and illegal construction on floodplains.
    *   Three major rivers flooded simultaneously for the first time, overwhelming "Pakistan's breadbasket" and displacing millions.
    *   Impact: Ongoing humanitarian crisis, severe impact on food security, highlights need for climate adaptation and governance reform.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgmx2e3nnw0o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgmx2e3nnw0o))

*   **Serbia Grapples with Aftermath of Train Station Collapse**

    *   Students have taken a leading role in demonstrations, demanding fresh elections and a technocratic government to address cronyism.
    *   Impact: Continued political instability and public distrust in government, with criminal charges filed against 13 individuals, including a former minister.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **China Loosens Chip Export Ban to Europe**
    *   Beijing will consider exempting individual firms from its chip export ban to Europe, imposed after Dutch authorities took over Chinese-owned chipmaker Nexperia.
    *   European automakers like Volvo and Volkswagen had warned of potential production stoppages if the ban remained.
    *   Impact: Potential easing of global supply chain disruptions for critical components, especially in the automotive sector, following US-China trade discussions.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly44qwgnx0o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly44qwgnx0o))

*   **Nvidia Strikes Major AI Deals in Asia, Navigates US-China Tensions**
    *   Nvidia will supply over 260,000 advanced AI chips to South Korea's government and tech giants like Samsung, LG, and Hyundai for various industrial applications.
    *   CEO Jensen Huang expressed disappointment over Nvidia's 0% share in China's AI business due to US export controls, hoping for new policies.
    *   Impact: Significant expansion of global AI infrastructure, but highlights the ongoing geopolitical friction in the semiconductor industry.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cyv862r7l2ro](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cyv862r7l2ro))

*   **Apple Reports Strong iPhone Sales Amidst Supply & Tariff Challenges**
    *   Apple announced a "tremendous response" globally for its latest iPhones (Air, 17 models), projecting a record holiday quarter revenue up to 12% higher year-on-year.
    *   The company narrowly missed Q4 iPhone sales estimates due to supply constraints and a lag in Chinese shipments, also noting a $1.1 billion tariff hit.
    *   Impact: Continued strong performance in the premium smartphone market, but lingering vulnerabilities to global supply chain issues and international trade policies.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz6n98exv9zo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz6n98exv9zo))

*   **Virgin Cleared to Challenge Eurostar in Channel Tunnel**
    *   The UK regulator approved Virgin Trains' application to share a depot with Eurostar, paving the way for Virgin to launch competing cross-Channel services from 2030.
    *   This decision could end Eurostar's 30-year monopoly, potentially leading to increased competition, lower fares, and more connectivity.
    *   Impact: Significant shift in the UK-European rail market, potentially benefiting consumers and driving investment in cross-border transport.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy8v5z15551o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy8v5z15551o))
