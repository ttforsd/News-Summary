## Major regional Events

*   **Gaza Conflict: Devastation Continues Amidst Fragile Ceasefire**
    *   Gaza City is described as a scene of "total devastation" after two years of war.
    *   Israeli forces report daily clashes with Hamas along the "yellow line," a temporary boundary.
    *   Hamas returned the body of Tanzanian hostage Joshua Mollel as part of a US-brokered ceasefire deal.
    *   Six deceased hostages remain in Gaza, with Israel accusing Hamas of delaying recovery efforts.
    *   More than 68,000 Gazans have been killed, according to the Hamas-run Health Ministry.
    *   The US is pushing for a second phase of its peace plan, including an international stabilization force.

*   **Sudan's Deepening Humanitarian Crisis**
    *   Sudan's paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF) agreed to a US-proposed humanitarian ceasefire after seizing el-Fasher.
    *   The 18-month siege of el-Fasher led to famine conditions, prompting international backlash over reported mass killings.
    *   A network of community kitchens, a lifeline for millions, is on the verge of collapse due to shortages and lack of funding.
    *   Over 24 million people face acute food shortages, in what the UN calls the world's largest humanitarian crisis.
    *   *Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74070y9e3go](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74070y9e3go)), ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgkl4nx784o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgkl4nx784o))*

*   **Typhoon Kalmaegi Devastates Southeast Asia, Climate Link Highlighted**

    *   Vietnam deployed 260,000 soldiers for rescue; six airports were closed.

    *   A study indicated climate change made Hurricane Melissa's rapid intensification six times more likely.
    *   Climate change increased Melissa's extreme rainfall by 16% and wind speeds by 7%.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **US Government Shutdown Causes Flight Disruptions**
    *   The US Transportation Secretary announced a 10% cut in flight capacity at 40 major airports starting Friday.
    *   This measure is due to air traffic controller fatigue amid the ongoing government shutdown.
    *   Airlines are waiving cancellation fees; the shutdown is the longest in US history, with federal workers unpaid.
    *   *Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2kp8dex14xo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2kp8dex14xo)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/faa-announces-flight-reductions-at-40-airports.-heres-where-cuts-are-expected-and-what-travelers-need-to-know.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/faa-announces-flight-reductions-at-40-airports.-heres-where-cuts-are-expected-and-what-travelers-need-to-know.html))*

*   **UK Monetary Policy & Wage Outlook**
    *   The Bank of England held interest rates steady but signaled future cuts, as inflation trends disinflationary.
    *   Governor Bailey indicated policy is "past peak-restrictiveness;" economists predict a potential Christmas rate cut.
    *   UK Chancellor is expected to announce a minimum wage hike to £12.70 by April 2026 in the Autumn Budget.
    *   This wage increase could lead to job losses and higher inflation, impacting graduate salaries.
    *   *Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/bank-of-england-rate-cut-a-close-call-ahead-of-the-autumn-budget.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/bank-of-england-rate-cut-a-close-call-ahead-of-the-autumn-budget.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/05/uk-exchange-newsletter-minimum-wage-hike-with-maximum-consequences.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/05/uk-exchange-newsletter-minimum-wage-hike-with-maximum-consequences.html))*

*   **US Labor Market Sees Significant Job Cuts**
    *   US job cuts in October surged to 153,074, the highest for the month in 22 years.
    *   This marks a 183% increase from September and the worst year for layoffs since 2009.
    *   The technology sector led cuts, attributed to AI integration and industry restructuring.
    *   Private payrolls rose by 42,000 in October, exceeding expectations and countering earlier labor market fears.
    *   *Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/job-cuts-in-october-hit-highest-level-for-the-month-in-22-years-challenger-says.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/job-cuts-in-october-hit-highest-level-for-the-month-in-22-years-challenger-says.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/05/private-payrolls-rose-42000-in-october-more-than-expected-and-countering-labor-market-fears-adp-says.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/05/private-payrolls-rose-42000-in-october-more-than-expected-and-countering-labor-market-fears-adp-says.html))*

*   **Global Trade & Shipping Demonstrate Resilience**
    *   Shipping giant Maersk raised its full-year profit outlook due to increasing container volumes.
    *   CEO cited "resilience of demand" globally, including a pickup in the US market.
    *   Dismissed concerns about the "death of globalization," noting strong manufacturing in China.

*   **Retail Sector Grapples with Tariff-Driven Price Hikes**
    *   Major retailers like Amazon, Walmart, and Target have increased prices this year due to Trump's tariffs.

    *   Third-party sellers on Amazon are particularly vulnerable to these tariff-driven costs.
    *   Tariffs are estimated to contribute to core inflation, impacting consumer spending.
    *   *Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/05/amazon-target-walmart-raising-prices-tariffs.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/05/amazon-target-walmart-raising-prices-tariffs.html))*
