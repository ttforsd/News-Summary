## Major regional Events

*   **Gaza Reconstruction Efforts Stalled by Political Impasse**
    *   Detail: Plans for rebuilding Gaza, estimated at $70bn, face deep disagreements among international, regional, and local actors.
    *   Context: Gaza City remains largely destroyed two years after war, with 60 million tonnes of rubble and active conflict hindering recovery.
    *   Impact: Gazans are skeptical of foreign-imposed schemes, with no clear path or funding secured for the decades-long process.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj41y42qxq8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj41y42qxq8o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0rp4lrdv8ko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0rp4lrdv8ko))
*   **Typhoon Kalmaegi Leaves Over 190 Dead Across Southeast Asia**
    *   Detail: Typhoon Kalmaegi killed at least 188 in the Philippines and five in Vietnam, now heading towards Cambodia and Laos.
    *   Context: The storm brought winds up to 149km/h, causing widespread flooding, property damage, and evacuations.
    *   Impact: Philippines declared a state of calamity; central Vietnam already suffered record rainfall.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c70jnx9e414o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c70jnx9e414o))
*   **Cameroon President Sworn in for Eighth Term Amid Unrest**
    *   Detail: Paul Biya, 92, the world's oldest president, began his eighth consecutive term, promising order after post-election violence.
    *   Context: Opposition accused authorities of fraud; protests led to at least 14 deaths and 1,200 arrests.
    *   Impact: Biya's continued rule despite challenges to electoral legitimacy raises stability concerns.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj978zlgp7go](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj978zlgp7go))
*   **Afghan Opium Cultivation Plummets After Taliban Ban**
    *   Detail: UN reports a significant drop in opium farming since the 2022 Taliban ban, with cultivation shrinking 20%.
    *   Context: Afghanistan previously produced over 80% of global opium; ban is mostly observed despite economic hardship.
    *   Impact: Decline in opium, but synthetic drug trafficking (e.g., methamphetamine) has increased.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5ypq5pdyk8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5ypq5pdyk8o))
*   **Hamas Returns Body of Deceased Tanzanian Hostage**
    *   Detail: Israel confirmed Hamas handed over the remains of Joshua Mollel, a 21-year-old Tanzanian student killed in October 2023.
    *   Context: This is part of the US-brokered Gaza ceasefire deal; six deceased hostages remain in Gaza.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **China's October Exports Unexpectedly Contract**
    *   Detail: Outbound shipments dropped 1.1% year-on-year, the first contraction since March 2024.
    *   Context: Exports to the US plunged 25%; imports rose only 1%, missing expectations due to weak domestic demand.
    *   Impact: Signals persistent challenges for China's economy despite a recent US-China trade truce.
    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/07/chinas-exports-october-trade-data.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/07/chinas-exports-october-trade-data.html))
*   **US Airports to Cut Thousands of Flights Due to Government Shutdown**
    *   Detail: Up to 10% of flights at 40 major airports will be reduced, affecting 3,500-4,000 daily flights.
    *   Context: Air traffic controllers are experiencing fatigue, working without pay amid the longest US government shutdown.
    *   Impact: Significant travel disruptions and safety concerns, with economic repercussions for the airline industry.
    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/faa-announces-flight-reductions-at-40-airports.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/faa-announces-flight-reductions-at-40-airports.html))

    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/tesla-shareholders-musk-pay.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/06/tesla-shareholders-musk-pay.html))
