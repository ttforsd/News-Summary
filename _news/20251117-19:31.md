## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **UN Security Council to Vote on Gaza Peace Plan**
    *   The US submitted a draft resolution proposing an International Stabilization Force (ISF) and transitional governance for Gaza.
    *   The plan includes disarming non-state armed groups like Hamas and establishing a newly trained Palestinian police force.
    *   Hamas has rejected the draft, calling it "dangerous" and an "attempt to subject the Gaza Strip to international authority." (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czr1dxzrx3zo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czr1dxzrx3zo))
*   **US to Aid South Korea in Nuclear Submarine Development**
    *   The US approved South Korea's plan to build nuclear-powered attack submarines and cooperate on fuel sourcing.
    *   Aimed at countering North Korea's nuclear submarine program and China's naval activity in the region.
    *   South Korea, already a civilian nuclear energy powerhouse, seeks to strengthen defense capabilities and regional standing. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c620qppzlgwo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c620qppzlgwo))
*   **US-India Energy Trade Deepens Amid Tariff Strain**
    *   India will import nearly 10% of its liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) from the US via a new one-year deal.
    *   This aims to diversify India's energy sourcing from the Middle East and reduce a $40 billion trade surplus with the US.
    *   US crude imports to India sharply rose in October, reaching the highest level since February 2021. (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/17/india-deepens-us-energy-trade-to-mend-trade-relations-amid-tariffs.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/17/india-deepens-us-energy-trade-to-mend-trade-relations-amid-tariffs.html))
*   **US Reaches Trade Deal with Switzerland**

    *   The deal, though welcomed by industry, faces criticism in Switzerland as a "surrender agreement." (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/17/we-havent-sold-our-soul-to-the-devil-swiss-defend-us-trade-deal.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/17/we-havent-sold-our-soul-to-the-devil-swiss-defend-us-trade-deal.html))

## Major Regional Events

*   **Bangladesh's Former PM Sheikh Hasina Sentenced to Death**
    *   A special tribunal in Dhaka convicted Sheikh Hasina of crimes against humanity and issued a death sentence in absentia.
    *   She was found responsible for ordering a deadly crackdown on student-led protests in 2024, killing up to 1,400.
    *   Hasina, in self-imposed exile, called the tribunal a "farce" and politically motivated. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cg3ee303yxpo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cg3ee303yxpo))
*   **Nigeria School Abduction:**
    *   Armed men killed a teacher and abducted at least 25 girls from a secondary school in Kebbi State.
    *   This marks the first major mass school abduction in Nigeria in over a year, highlighting persistent security crises.
    *   Police and military forces have launched a search and rescue operation in surrounding forests. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5ypn7pwgv5o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5ypn7pwgv5o))
*   **Tanzanian Police Used Lethal Force in Election Protests**
    *   Verified footage shows Tanzanian police firing live rounds and tear gas into crowds during widespread protests in October.
    *   The UN human rights office reports hundreds of deaths and injuries, while a diplomatic source cited at least 500 fatalities.
    *   President Samia Suluhu Hassan has called for an investigation and leniency for arrested protesters. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3vn4rlqd52o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3vn4rlqd52o))
*   **Ecuador Votes Against Foreign Military Bases**
    *   Voters rejected changing the constitution to allow foreign military bases, frustrating US hopes for expanded presence.
    *   President Daniel Noboa advocated for the change to combat rising organized crime and drug trafficking.
    *   The decision impacts US counter-narcotics efforts in the eastern Pacific region. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c891zkwvjl2o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c891zkwvjl2o))
*   **Brazil's Amazon Rainforest Protections Under Threat**
    *   A long-standing ban (Amazon Soy Moratorium) on soya grown on deforested land after 2008 faces legal challenge from farming interests.
    *   Environmental groups warn lifting the ban could trigger a new wave of deforestation, pushing the Amazon toward a "tipping point."
    *   The dispute divides the Brazilian government, with some ministries defending the moratorium for its environmental benefits. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwypzdgwg1yo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwypzdgwg1yo))
*   **Chile Presidential Election Heads to Run-off**
    *   A Communist Party candidate and a far-right rival will face off in December after no clear winner in the first round.
    *   The campaign focused heavily on crime and immigration amidst a growing influx of migrants, particularly from Venezuela.
    *   This shift could see Chile become the latest Latin American country to move to the right politically. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0jd0v8dvpwo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0jd0v8dvpwo))
*   **Australia Revokes Visa of Neo-Nazi Rally Participant**
    *   Australia cancelled the visa of a South African man who attended an anti-Jewish protest in New South Wales.
    *   The government has a strict stance against hatred, and the Nazi salute is punishable by prison time.
    *   This action reflects Australia's efforts to counter a recent rise in right-wing extremism. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj417gqq5n1o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj417gqq5n1o))
*   **Canada's Oil Pipeline Feud Impacts Global Ambitions**
    *   An inter-provincial dispute between Alberta and British Columbia over a new oil pipeline to the Pacific is hindering Canada's goal to diversify energy exports to Asia.
    *   Alberta's government is drafting a proposal, but British Columbia opposes it due to environmental concerns and impact on its LNG export plans.
    *   The federal government is attempting to mediate but may consider reviving a pipeline project to the US as an alternative. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgkz42yz35do](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgkz42yz35do))
*   **India's Air Quality Index Criticized for 500-Point Cap**
    *   Official air quality index (AQI) in northern India caps at 500, despite private monitors showing far higher, more toxic levels.
    *   Experts argue the cap, set over a decade ago, misrepresents actual pollution and needs revision as health impacts worsen beyond 500.
    *   Differences in measurement tools and pollutant thresholds also contribute to discrepancies with international standards. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ced6jgg0180o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ced6jgg0180o))
