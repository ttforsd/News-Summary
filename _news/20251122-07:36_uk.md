## Major regional Events

*   **Ukraine Peace Plan at G20 Under Scrutiny**
    *   Ukraine's allies are discussing a leaked US peace plan at the G20, which proposes Kyiv cede eastern territories, reduce its army, and pledge non-NATO membership.
    *   Ukrainian President Zelensky called it "one of the most difficult moments," facing pressure to accept terms seen as favorable to Moscow or risk losing US support.
    *   European leaders expressed nervousness, with UK PM Starmer aiming to "strengthen" the plan for future negotiations.
    *   Russian President Putin acknowledged the plan as a potential "basis" for settlement, indicating willingness for flexibility.
    *   The 28-point proposal also suggested Russia's reintegration into the global economy, including sanctions lifting and a return to the G8.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyx9xnpll8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyx9xnpll8o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cde6yld78d6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cde6yld78d6o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y9k0mrm2ro](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y9k0mrm2ro), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c98n5d4nyv6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c98n5d4nyv6o))
*   **UN Climate Summit Struggles with Fossil Fuel Deal**
    *   COP30 climate talks in Belém, Brazil, extended beyond deadline due to a fierce dispute over the future of fossil fuels.
    *   Oil-producing countries (including Russia and Saudi Arabia) and emerging economies are resisting stronger commitments to reduce fossil fuel use.
    *   Developing nations are demanding financial aid from richer countries to cope with climate change impacts before agreeing to stricter targets.
    *   A draft agreement removed any mention of fossil fuels, prompting concern from countries like the UK.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0q5ewew4geo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0q5ewew4geo))
*   **US Deporting Iranian Christian Converts**
    *   The US is deporting Iranian Christian converts back to Iran, despite asylum claims and risks of persecution.
    *   Deportees report sensitive religious and political information from their asylum files was not removed before removal.
    *   Christian converts face arrest, interrogation, and prison in Iran, often branded as "Zionist Christianity" agents.
    *   The White House stated all asylum claims were adjudicated and deportations involved final orders or voluntary departures.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdege0k962do](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdege0k962do))
*   **Former UK Politician Jailed for Pro-Russia Bribery**
    *   Nathan Gill, former leader of Reform UK in Wales and an ex-MEP, received a 10.5-year sentence for accepting up to £40,000 in bribes.
    *   Bribes were for promoting pro-Russian views in Ukraine, undermining democratic institutions.
    *   Met Police described Gill as an "extraordinarily willing participant" and a "threat to national security."
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yd878ejqko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yd878ejqko), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c98n5d4nyv6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c98n5d4nyv6o))
*   **Serbian President Denies Bosnia "Sniper Tourism" Allegations**
    *   Serbian President Aleksandar Vucic refuted claims of involvement in "sniper tourism" during the 1990s siege of Sarajevo.
    *   Allegations, made by a Croatian journalist, suggest Vucic was a "war volunteer" with Bosnian-Serb forces.
    *   Vucic called the accusations "malicious disinformation" and stated he never participated in combat.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c246pv2n25zo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c246pv2n25zo))

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **AI Market Bubble Concerns Persist Amid Tech Giants' Spending**
    *   Google CEO Sundar Pichai acknowledged "elements of irrationality" in the AI investment boom, despite Google's $90bn annual investment.
    *   Concerns about an "AI bubble" persist, highlighted by market concentration in a few tech giants comprising a third of the S&P 500.
    *   PwC's global chairman warns AI could lead to fewer entry-level graduate jobs, while the firm struggles to find AI engineers.
