## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **UK Economic Outlook and AI Market Risks**
    *   The OECD forecasts UK economic growth to slow to 1.4% this year and 1.2% in 2026, citing tax increases and government spending cuts.
        *   UK inflation is expected to remain the highest in the G7 at 3.5%.
        *   Globally, the economic outlook remains "fragile," with risks from trade barriers and potential AI bubble burst.
    *   The Bank of England issued a warning about a "sharp correction" in the valuations of major tech companies, particularly in the AI sector.
        *   AI growth is largely fueled by trillions of dollars in debt, posing financial stability risks if valuations decline.
        *   The Bank concurrently reduced capital requirements for UK banks to encourage lending and economic growth.

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Cosmetic Face Filler Safety Warnings**
    *   Experts have issued stark warnings about severe complications arising from cosmetic face filler injections.
        *   Risks include blocked arteries, potentially leading to skin loss, facial deformity, and even blindness.
        *   Ultrasound scans of 100 problematic cases revealed absent blood flow in nearly half.
        *   Clinics are now advised to use ultrasound for pre-injection planning and targeted treatment of complications; the UK government plans stricter regulations.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c773n331055o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c773n331055o))
*   **Prostate Cancer Screening Recommendations (UK)**
    *   The UK National Screening Committee recommended against widespread prostate cancer screening for most men.
        *   Regular screening is advised only for men with specific BRCA genetic mutations due to higher risk.
        *   The decision reflects a balance between potential lives saved and the harms of overdiagnosis and unnecessary treatments.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm20169gz44o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm20169gz44o))
*   **Novel Rectal Cancer Treatment Approved**
    *   NICE approved Papillon, a new low-energy X-ray treatment for rectal cancer, for use in the UK.
        *   This method offers an organ-preserving alternative to surgery for eligible patients, improving quality of life.
        *   Trials demonstrated 93% organ preservation in cases of small tumors.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c93n4qe9xeko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c93n4qe9xeko))
*   **New Insights into Brain Development**
    *   A University of Cambridge study revealed the human brain undergoes five distinct phases, extending adolescence into the early 30s.
        *   Key developmental turning points were identified at ages nine, 32, 66, and 83.
        *   These findings could enhance understanding of mental health disorders and dementia risks across the lifespan.
