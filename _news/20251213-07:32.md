**Daily Global Briefing: December 13, 2025**

## Executive summary

*   **Geopolitical Tensions:** US-led peace efforts are struggling in Ukraine and DR Congo, while fighting continues between Thailand and Cambodia. Eritrea's withdrawal from a regional bloc heightens tensions in East Africa.
*   **Ukraine War Impact:** Russia continues to target Ukraine's medical infrastructure, causing significant losses in essential supplies.
*   **US Domestic Policy:** President Trump is set to reclassify marijuana and has named a top candidate for Federal Reserve chair, signaling potential shifts.
*   **AI Market Volatility:** The AI sector experienced market "angst," with major tech stocks like Broadcom and Oracle facing declines despite strong demand.
*   **Economic Indicators:** The UK economy unexpectedly shrank, while India's inflation rose, showing mixed global economic signals.
*   **Tech & IP:** Disney invests in OpenAI and will allow characters for AI video generation, amidst ongoing debates on intellectual property.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **Ukraine Peace Talks & Russian Resilience:**
    *   US envoy Steve Witkoff will meet Ukrainian President Zelensky and European leaders in Berlin.
    *   Goal: Discuss the latest peace agreement, with the US pushing for a deal by Christmas.
    *   Key sticking points remain: territorial disputes and Ukraine's security guarantees.
    *   A former Russian central bank official stated Russia can fund the war for at least 2-3 more years.
    *   NATO warned Europe to prepare for potential Russian military action within five years.
    *   Russia's central bank plans to sue Euroclear over frozen assets; EU considers using them for Ukraine's aid.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0l9954yr9ko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0l9954yr9ko), [https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/12/ukraine-war-trump-frustrated-amid-warning-putin-can-escalate-conflict.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/12/ukraine-war-trump-frustrated-amid-warning-putin-can-escalate-conflict.html))

*   **Gaza Ceasefire Stalls:**
    *   A US-imposed ceasefire in Gaza remains in its initial phase, with displacement and rubble widespread.
    *   Israeli PM Netanyahu requires the return of all missing hostages before advancing to the next peace stage.
    *   Heavy rainfall and flooding exacerbated the humanitarian crisis, affecting 800,000 Gazans.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2lvplrxpe9o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2lvplrxpe9o))

*   **Venezuela's Diminished Alliances:**
    *   Venezuela's key allies, China and Russia, are showing limited concrete support amid heightened US pressure.
    *   The US deployed forces in the Caribbean, conducted strikes, and seized an oil tanker.
    *   Experts cite Russia's Ukraine conflict and US-China diplomatic efforts as reasons for reduced backing.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c075mr3190mo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c075mr3190mo))

*   **Eritrea's Withdrawal from IGAD:**
    *   Eritrea withdrew from the East African regional bloc Igad, accusing it of acting against its interests.
    *   This decision comes amid rising tensions with Ethiopia over access to the Red Sea.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly555k8de3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly555k8de3o))

## Major Regional Events

*   **Southeast Asia Border Conflict:**
    *   Fighting between Thai and Cambodian forces continues despite US President Trump's ceasefire announcement.
    *   Thailand's PM stated a ceasefire is conditional on Cambodian withdrawal and landmine removal.
    *   The conflict has led to 21 deaths and the evacuation of 700,000 people.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd0kkyx3vvxo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd0kkyx3vvxo))

*   **DR Congo Peace Deal Collapse:**
    *   M23 rebels seized Uvira, a key city in DR Congo, shattering a US-brokered peace agreement.
    *   The US Ambassador to the UN accused Rwanda of fueling regional conflict by supporting the M23.
    *   Around 50,000 people have fled into neighboring Burundi due to the escalating violence.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gk34k9k23o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gk34k9k23o))

*   **Iranian Nobel Laureate Arrested:**

    *   The Nobel Committee expressed concern, demanding clarification of her whereabouts and release.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly559exm87o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly555k8de3o))

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **AI Sector Stock Volatility:**
    *   Broadcom shares dropped 11% despite strong earnings, reflecting "AI angst" among investors.
    *   Oracle, Nvidia, and Advanced Micro Devices also experienced declines, impacting tech markets.

    *   Oracle denies reports of delays in its data center commitments for OpenAI.
