## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Bank of Japan Raises Rates to 30-Year High:**
    *   The Bank of Japan increased its short-term rates by 25 basis points to 0.75%, the highest level since 1995.
    *   Detail: This move follows 44 consecutive months of inflation above the 2% target. The 10-year JGB yield rose past 2%.
    *   Impact: Signals a shift towards policy normalization, though it risks exacerbating a downturn in the Japanese economy.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/19/bank-of-japan-boj-rate-cpi-inflation-takaichi-ueda.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/19/bank-of-japan-boj-rate-cpi-inflation-takaichi-ueda.html))
*   **European Central Banks Conclude Rate Decisions:**
    *   The ECB, Norges Bank, and Riksbank held their interest rates, while the Bank of England made a cut.
    *   Detail: The BoE's cut was by a narrow 5-4 margin, indicating caution on inflation risks. ECB projections upgraded growth outlook, justifying the hold.
    *   Impact: European monetary policies diverge, with hints of future rate hikes from ECB and Riksbank remaining on hold.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/takeaways-from-boe-ecb-norges-bank-riksbank-interest-rate-decisions.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/18/takeaways-from-boe-ecb-norges-bank-riksbank-interest-rate-decisions.html))
