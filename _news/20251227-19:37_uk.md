## Major regional Events

*   **Ukraine Faces Renewed Russian Aggression Ahead of Peace Talks.**
    *   Kyiv endured an intense Russian missile and drone attack, killing two and injuring 32.
    *   Forty percent of residential buildings in Kyiv were left without heating.
    *   President Zelensky condemned the attack as Russia showing "doesn't want peace."
    *   This occurred as Zelensky prepared for talks with US President Trump on a new 20-point peace plan.
    *   Russia's Defense Ministry stated targets were energy facilities linked to Ukraine's military.
    *   Poland scrambled fighter jets, but no airspace violation was confirmed.
    *   Russia claimed intercepting 111 Ukrainian drones over its own regions.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1dzvpg1341o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1dzvpg1341o))

*   **Thailand and Cambodia Agree to Border Ceasefire.**
    *   A ceasefire took effect, aiming to end three weeks of deadly clashes.
    *   Nearly one million people were displaced by the conflict.
    *   Agreement includes freezing front lines, banning reinforcements, and civilian return.
    *   Eighteen Cambodian soldiers held by Thailand are to be released after 72 hours.
    *   Thai Defence Minister called the agreement a "test" of sincerity.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0q5je8048xo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0q5je8048xo))

*   **Malaysian Police Found Responsible for Pastor's Abduction.**
    *   Malaysia's High Court ruled that the police Special Branch abducted Pastor Raymond Koh in 2017.
    *   The ruling holds the police and government responsible for the enforced disappearance.
    *   Investigations linked police to the abduction due to alleged anti-Islam activities by the pastor.
    *   Koh's wife won a civil lawsuit for his whereabouts and significant compensation.
    *   The government is appealing the verdict, which could lead to Malaysia's largest payout.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx276v1yvryo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx276v1yvryo))

*   **UK Launches Military "Gap Year" Scheme for Recruitment.**
    *   The government introduced a "gap year" program for under-25s to join the Army, Royal Navy, or RAF.
    *   Aims to address long-term recruitment and retention challenges in the armed forces.
    *   Initial intake targets 150 recruits, with plans to expand to 1,000 annually.
    *   Recruits will not be deployed on active operations, focusing on skills training.
    *   Opposition criticized the small numbers as insufficient for "war readiness."
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62dyp9pgd2o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62dyp9pgd2o))

*   **Italy Arrests Nine for Alleged Hamas Financing.**
    *   Italian police arrested nine people, including a Palestinian association president.
    *   They are accused of raising €7 million for Hamas, disguised as humanitarian aid.
    *   Funds were allegedly diverted to Hamas's military wing and families of militants.
    *   The investigation began after the October 7, 2023, Hamas attack on Israel.
    *   Over €8 million in assets were seized during the operation.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqxq59np394o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqxq59np394o))

*   **US Judge Blocks Detention of British Social Media Campaigner.**
    *   A US judge issued a temporary order blocking the detention of Imran Ahmed.
    *   Ahmed, founder of the Center for Countering Digital Hate, was denied a US visa.
    *   The US accused him of coercing tech platforms into censoring free speech.
    *   European leaders condemned the US action as "intimidation" and "attack on free speech."
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c33mx6j5jrvo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c33mx6j5jrvo))

*   **UK Hospitals Face Post-Strike Recovery.**
    *   English hospitals are recovering from a five-day strike by resident doctors.
    *   Thousands of elective and outpatient appointments were canceled during the dispute.
    *   The BMA is pushing for a "proper fix" for pay and job security.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **TUC Urges Labour Not to Rule Out EU Customs Union.**
    *   TUC leader Paul Nowak advocated for "closest possible economic and political relationship" with the EU.
    *   He argued an EU customs union is "essential" for UK economic growth and reducing trade barriers.
    *   Labour PM Keir Starmer has ruled out rejoining to pursue independent global trade deals.
    *   Thirteen Labour MPs recently supported a bill for customs union negotiations.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2dj27y0z0o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2dj27y0z0o))

*   **US Economy Posts Strongest Growth in Two Years.**
    *   The US economy expanded at an annual rate of 4.3% in Q3, exceeding expectations.
    *   Growth was largely driven by a 3.5% jump in consumer spending and 7.4% surge in exports.
    *   Analysts foresee continued momentum into 2026, supported by tax cuts and interest rate adjustments.
    *   Persistent inflation, however, continues to impact lower and middle-income households.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62n9ynzrdpo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62n9ynzrdpo))

*   **US Federal Reserve Chair Nomination on the Horizon.**
    *   President Trump is expected to name a new Federal Reserve chair to replace Jerome Powell.
    *   The choice will influence US monetary policy direction amid political and internal disagreements.
    *   Top contenders include Kevin Hassett, Kevin Warsh, and current governor Christopher Waller.
    *   All leading candidates have expressed support for lower interest rates.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g907zekllo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g907zekllo))

*   **UK Boxing Day Sales See Muted Performance.**
    *   Boxing Day retail activity showed a subdued start compared to previous years.
    *   High street visits were down 1.5%, and shopping centers saw a 0.6% decline from 2024.
    *   Overall sales spend is forecasted to fall to £3.6 billion from £4.6 billion last year.
    *   Consumer cost-consciousness and earlier pre-Christmas discounting are key factors.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c865d7zw26jo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c865d7zw26jo))
