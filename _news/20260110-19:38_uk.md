## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Quantum Computing Advances Rapidly**
    *   Google's "Willow" quantum computer in Santa Barbara achieved significant speed breakthroughs.
    *   Solved a benchmark problem in minutes that would take classical computers "trillions of trillions" of years.
    *   Impact: Potential to revolutionize drug discovery, energy production, financial security, and decrypt sensitive data (e.g., Bitcoin).
    *   A global race for quantum supremacy is intensifying, with China investing heavily ($15bn).
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62r6dvpl5ro](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62r6dvpl5ro)

*   **OpenAI Launches ChatGPT Health in US**
    *   OpenAI introduced a new ChatGPT feature in the US for analyzing personal medical records.
    *   Aims to provide personalized health advice using data from apps like Apple Health, MyFitnessPal, and user-provided medical records.
    *   OpenAI states robust privacy measures: separate storage, no AI training from health chats, not for diagnosis.
    *   Impact: Raises significant privacy concerns among campaigners regarding sensitive health information.
    *   Not available in the UK, EU, or Switzerland due to stricter data protection regulations.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpqy29d0yjgo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpqy29d0yjgo)

*   **UK Health System Under Winter Pressure**
    *   Flu cases are rising again in the UK post-Christmas gatherings, with a 9% increase in hospitalizations.
    *   The "super flu" (H3N2 "subclade K") is circulating, to which the population has lower immunity.
    *   Colder weather is contributing to increased slips, falls, and respiratory conditions.
    *   Hospitals are experiencing "corridor care" due to a lack of beds, raising patient safety concerns.
    *   Impact: Calls for eligible individuals to get flu vaccines remain urgent to alleviate pressure.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9qp47ze35do](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9qp47ze35do), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crk7j8nxlr6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crk7j8nxlr6o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj0nz9eldnzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj0nz9eldnzo)

    *   The personal story of a BBC journalist living with Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA) and a celebrity's twin diagnosis highlights advances in treatment and calls for broader screening.
    *   Scotland is implementing routine SMA screening for newborns, with a UK-wide review underway.
