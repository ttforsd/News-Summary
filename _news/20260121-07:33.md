## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **Greenland Dispute and Transatlantic Tensions**
    *   US President Trump reaffirmed his intent to acquire Greenland, threatening 10% tariffs on eight European countries opposing the move.
    *   Greenland's government rejected the takeover, with its Prime Minister stating the island must be prepared for "everything."
    *   European leaders condemned Trump's tariff threats, with France's President Macron warning of a "world without rules."
    *   The EU is considering retaliatory measures, including suspending approval of a US trade deal and using an "Anti-Coercion Instrument."
    *   Russia views these escalating tensions positively, seeing it as weakening the Western alliance.
    *   A former Trump adviser suggested the demands might be a negotiation tactic for increased military presence and access to rare earth minerals, ruling out military invasion.
    *   The UK is also facing Trump's criticism for its deal to transfer Chagos Islands sovereignty to Mauritius, which he called an "act of great stupidity."
