## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US "Board of Peace" and "New Gaza" Initiative**
    *   President Trump launched a new "Board of Peace" at Davos to address the Israel-Hamas war and oversee Gaza's reconstruction.
    *   The US presented a "Master Plan" for "New Gaza," including skyscrapers and economic zones.
    *   The plan outlines a new seaport/airport and a security perimeter along the Egyptian/Israeli borders.
    *   Hamas is expected to demilitarize and return the last Israeli hostage's body.
    *   Members include Bahrain, Morocco, Argentina, Hungary, Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE.
    *   Several key US allies (Canada, UK, France, Germany, Italy) were absent or rejected invitations, some citing concerns over Russia's potential involvement.
    *   Somaliland's President met with his Israeli counterpart and Eric Trump, discussing stronger ties and investment after Israel's recognition of Somaliland.

    *   Negotiators from Russia, Ukraine, and the US met for their first trilateral talks since the 2022 invasion.

    *   Ukraine's President Zelenskyy seeks continued US support, particularly for air defense.
    *   US President Trump is actively pushing for a peace deal, though his reliability as a guarantor is questioned.

## Major regional Events

*   **Syrian Government Advances on Kurdish-led Forces**
    *   President Sharaa's forces reclaimed large territories in north-eastern Syria from the Syrian Democratic Forces (SDF).
    *   This marks the most significant shift in control since the fall of the Assad regime in 2024.
    *   A new 14-point deal mandates SDF integration into the Syrian army and government control of oil/gas fields.
    *   The US stance, seen as supporting Sharaa, is perceived as a betrayal by Syrian Kurds.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9311l0d40qo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9311l0d40qo)

*   **Human Rights Concerns in Yemen and Iran**
    *   The BBC gained access to alleged UAE-run secret prisons in Yemen, confirming torture and abuse claims.
    *   Former detainees reported beatings, sexual abuse, and prolonged detention without due process.
    *   In Iran, an eyewitness described a violent crackdown on protests, with security forces using live ammunition.
    *   An internet blackout in Iran has obscured the full extent of the violence and casualties.
    *   In Afghanistan, the Taliban released a 22-year-old female taekwondo athlete after 13 days in jail.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3r117w55q4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3r117w55q4o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9dvvzzpwylo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9dvvzzpwylo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1evv6dz3e9o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1evv6dz3e9o)

*   **Severe Winter Storm Hits US**
    *   Over 160 million Americans are preparing for an "extremely dangerous" winter storm.
    *   Forecasts predict heavy snow, freezing rain, sub-zero temperatures, and life-threatening hypothermia risks.
    *   Widespread travel disruption, delays, cancellations, and power outages are expected across many states.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0r44l05q52o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0r44l05q52o)

*   **Uganda's Internet Shutdown Impacts Economy and Rights**
    *   A government-ordered internet shutdown during elections paralyzed mobile money transactions.
    *   This significantly impacted incomes for many small businesses and individuals.
    *   The shutdown also hampered journalists' reporting and students' online learning.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2p54rm4k4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2p54rm4k4o)

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **US Inflation Remains Elevated**
    *   The Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) price index, the Fed's preferred inflation gauge, registered 2.8% in November.
    *   This figure remains above the central bank's 2% target.
    *   Consumer spending and personal income showed moderate increases, indicating continued economic expansion.
    *   Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/22/pce-inflation-november-2026.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/22/pce-inflation-november-2026.html)

*   **Bank of Japan Holds Rates, Raises Growth Forecasts**
    *   The Bank of Japan (BOJ) maintained its key interest rate at 0.75%, despite one board member advocating for a hike.
    *   Economic growth forecasts for fiscal years 2025 and 2026 were upgraded to 0.9% and 1% respectively.
    *   December inflation cooled to 2.1% (lowest since March 2022) but has been above the 2% target for 45 consecutive months.
    *   A snap election is scheduled for February 8, with Prime Minister Takaichi favoring monetary easing.
    *   Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/23/boj-rate-decision-snap-election-takaichi-gdp.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/23/boj-rate-decision-snap-election-takaichi-gdp.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/23/japan-december-inflation-rice-prices-loom-large-ahead-of-election.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/23/japan-december-inflation-rice-prices-loom-large-ahead-of-election.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/23/asia-markets-mostly-advance-as-bank-of-japan-leaves-interest-rates-on-hold.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/23/asia-markets-mostly-advance-as-bank-of-japan-leaves-interest-rates-on-hold.html)
