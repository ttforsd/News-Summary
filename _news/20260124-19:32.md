*   **US-Canada-China Trade Tensions Escalate**

    *   Threat issued if Canada proceeds with a trade deal with China, particularly on electric vehicles.
    *   Trump accused Canada of being a "Drop Off Port" for China to bypass US tariffs.
    *   Canadian PM Mark Carney had previously criticized "great powers" at Davos for economic coercion.
    *   US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent also commented on Alberta separatism, calling the province a "natural partner" for the US.

*   **US Shifts Global Defense Strategy**
    *   The Pentagon's new National Defense Strategy prioritizes US homeland and Western Hemisphere security.
    *   This marks a significant shift from previous focus on China as the top defense priority.
    *   Strategy calls for greater "burden-sharing" from allies in countering global threats.
    *   Europe is expected to take the lead on regional threats less severe for the US.
    *   Russia is described as a "persistent but manageable threat" to NATO's eastern members.
    *   The strategy re-affirms guaranteed US military and commercial access to Greenland.

## Major regional Events

*   **Ukraine Conflict & Failed Peace Talks**
    *   Two days of trilateral peace talks between Russia, Ukraine, and the US in Abu Dhabi ended without a breakthrough.
    *   Talks coincided with renewed Russian missile strikes on Ukraine's energy infrastructure.
    *   One person was killed and 35 injured in overnight attacks, damaging Kyiv's critical heating systems.
    *   6,000 apartment blocks in Kyiv were left without heating in sub-zero temperatures (-12C).
    *   Ukraine's reliance on vulnerable Soviet-era centralized heating systems exacerbates the impact.
    *   A second meeting is tentatively scheduled for next week; territorial concessions remain a significant obstacle.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwyr6p30054o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwyr6p30054o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c338jpk8r1vo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c338jpk8r1vo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/videos/c20dlr9kn37o](https://www.bbc.com/news/videos/c20dlr9kn37o)
*   **China Investigates Top Military Generals**
    *   China's defense ministry announced an investigation into General Zhang Youxia and General Liu Zhenli.
    *   Zhang Youxia, 75, is the highest-ranking general and a close ally of President Xi Jinping.
    *   The probe is for "grave violations of discipline and the law," a common euphemism for corruption.
    *   This continues Xi's extensive anti-corruption campaigns, often seen as a tool to consolidate power.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c701kr5zqdqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c701kr5zqdqo)
*   **Germany Arrests Suspected Hamas Member**
    *   German police arrested "Mohammad S," a Lebanese national, at Berlin's Brandenburg Airport.
    *   He is suspected of being a Hamas member and involved in planning attacks on Jewish and Israeli institutions in Europe.
    *   Authorities believe he helped procure ammunition in August 2025 and is linked to earlier arrests.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c8rm14lkkvzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c8rm14lkkvzo)
*   **Spain's Rail System Faces Crisis**
    *   Spain's high-speed rail network is in a deep crisis after two deadly accidents within days.
    *   A collision in Andalusia resulted in 45 fatalities.
    *   Separately, a trainee driver was killed near Barcelona when a wall collapsed onto the tracks during heavy rain.
    *   Train drivers' unions have called for a nationwide strike, demanding urgent safety guarantees and protesting network deterioration.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0keelnyrvro](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0keelnyrvro)
*   **Sudan's War Impacts Education and Civilians**
    *   Twin sisters in el-Obeid, Sudan, returned to their school where shelling in August 2024 killed 14 classmates and their teacher.
    *   The incident highlights the devastating human cost of Sudan's ongoing civil war.
    *   An estimated 13 million school-age children in Sudan are out of school, with many facilities converted to shelters for displaced people.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd9eeyz933jo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd9eeyz933jo)
*   **Allegations of UAE-Run Secret Prisons in Yemen**
    *   A BBC investigation confirmed long-standing allegations of secret detention facilities operated by the UAE and its allies in Yemen.
    *   Former detainees reported severe torture, including beatings, sexual abuse, and confinement in unventilated shipping containers.
    *   The Yemeni government now claims it was unaware of these prisons until recently liberating the areas from UAE control.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3r117w55q4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3r117w55q4o)
*   **Canada Boosts Arctic Defense Amid Geopolitical Competition**
    *   Canada's Prime Minister pledges increased investment and "boots on the ground" to defend its vast Arctic territory.
    *   This initiative is prompted by US pressure and growing military presence from Russia and China in the strategically vital region.
    *   Concerns exist over aging defense infrastructure and new threats like Russian hypersonic missiles.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9vx0p77pn8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9vx0p77pn8o)
