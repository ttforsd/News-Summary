## Major Regional Events

*   **Storm Chandra Impacts UK:**
    *   Widespread flooding, road closures, and transport cancellations affect large parts of the UK.
    *   Thousands of properties in England and Northern Ireland lost power.
    *   Met Office issued yellow warnings for wind, rain, and snow, with an amber warning for Northern Ireland.
    *   This is the third major storm to hit the UK in January, causing significant disruption.

*   **Iran Protester Crackdown:**
    *   Protesters recount security forces using lethal force during early January unrest.
    *   Human rights groups report over 6,000 confirmed deaths; authorities cite fewer deaths, mostly security personnel.
    *   Witnesses describe live rounds, pellet guns targeting faces; internet communications were severely disrupted.
    *   Families allegedly pressured to pay for bodies or attribute deaths to security forces.

*   **Spain to Legalize Undocumented Migrants:**
    *   Spain plans to grant legal status to approximately half a million undocumented migrants.
    *   Eligible individuals must have no criminal record and lived in Spain for five months by Dec 2025.
    *   Aims to promote human rights, integration, and support economic growth.
    *   Conservative parties criticize the move, fearing a "pull effect."

*   **UK-France Migrant Agreement Update:**

    *   350 migrants arrived in the UK via the approved route under the same deal.
    *   Home Office cited a "French side operational complication" for the disparity.
    *   Critics deem the scheme a "lamentable failure" given 41,000 Channel crossings last year.

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **TikTok Settles Social Media Addiction Lawsuit:**
    *   TikTok reached a settlement in a landmark social media addiction trial, avoiding court.
    *   Plaintiff alleged the platform's algorithms led to addiction and mental health issues.
    *   Meta (Instagram, Facebook) and Google (YouTube) remain defendants in the case.
    *   This marks a significant shift in legal challenges against tech product design.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c24g8v6qr1mo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c24g8v6qr1mo?at_medium=referral&at_campaign=rss))

*   **Pornhub Restricts UK Access:**
    *   Pornhub will restrict access for new UK users from February 2, citing age verification laws.
    *   Only existing account holders will be able to access content.
    *   Parent company Aylo claims the Online Safety Act drives users to "darker, unregulated corners."
    *   Ofcom states services must either implement robust age checks or block UK access.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czr428rxg57o?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czr428rxg57o?at_medium=referral&at_campaign=rss))

*   **Menopause Linked to Alzheimer's-like Brain Changes:**
    *   A large UK study suggests menopause is linked to brain changes similar to Alzheimer's disease.
    *   Researchers observed a loss of grey matter in areas crucial for memory and emotion.
    *   This may partly explain why women have a higher risk of dementia than men.
    *   Hormone replacement therapy (HRT) did not appear to prevent these changes in the study.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9qpp1g5ylvo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9qpp1g5ylvo?at_medium=referral&at_campaign=rss))

*   **Infant Formula Batch Recalled Over Toxin:**
    *   Danone recalls a specific batch of Aptamil First Infant Formula in the UK.
    *   The product is contaminated with cereulide, a toxin that can cause vomiting.
    *   Contamination originated from a shared third-party ingredient supplier.
    *   Parents are advised to cease use and seek medical advice if concerned.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1lz2m02rzzo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1lz2m02rzzo?at_medium=referral&at_campaign=rss))

*   **UK Loses Measles Elimination Status:**
    *   The World Health Organization (WHO) stripped the UK of its measles elimination status.
    *   Decision based on 3,600 suspected cases in 2024 and over 1,000 last year.
    *   Vaccination rates have fallen below the 95% herd immunity threshold.
    *   Public health agencies urge increased MMRV vaccine uptake, with NHS offering catch-up jabs.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgjw0xegxglo?at_medium=RSS&at_campaign=rss](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgjw0xegxglo?at_medium=referral&at_campaign=rss))
