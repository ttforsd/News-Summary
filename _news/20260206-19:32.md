## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Nuclear Talks Advance Amid Tensions**
    *   US and Iranian officials held mediated talks in Oman regarding Iran's nuclear program.
    *   Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi described discussions as a "good beginning" focused "exclusively on nuclear" issues.
    *   The US also sought to discuss Iran's ballistic missiles and support for regional groups, highlighting differing agendas.
    *   The US issued a security alert urging American citizens to "leave Iran now" ahead of these high-stakes talks.
    *   Impact: Despite initial progress on nuclear aspects, significant distrust and broad disagreements persist, maintaining conflict risks.

*   **US-Russia Nuclear Treaty Expires**
    *   The New START Treaty, the last arms control agreement limiting US and Russian nuclear warheads, has officially expired.
    *   This marks the first time since 1991 that there are no binding limits on the nuclear arsenals of the two largest nuclear powers.
    *   Impact: Raises international concerns about a potential new nuclear arms race and increased global instability.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/videos/c78e2g00llko](https://www.bbc.com/news/videos/c78e2g00llko))

*   **US-India Trade Deal Progresses with Boeing Orders**
    *   India is reportedly prepared to place up to $80 billion in Boeing aircraft orders following a US trade deal.
    *   Indian Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal indicated potential for $500 billion in US goods procurement over five years.
    *   US President Trump announced tariff reductions on Indian goods from 50% to 18%, with India also expected to lower tariffs on US goods to zero.
    *   Disagreements remain regarding India's agricultural market and cessation of Russian oil imports, which India is unlikely to fully commit to.
    *   Impact: Boosts US-India economic ties and Boeing, but specific terms face internal friction.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/06/india-buy-boeing-aircrafts-80-billion-trade-us-deal.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/06/india-buy-boeing-aircrafts-80-billion-trade-us-deal.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/05/cnbcs-inside-india-newsletter-the-facts-and-frictions-of-the-us-india-trade-deal.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/05/cnbcs-inside-india-newsletter-the-facts-and-frictions-of-the-us-india-trade-deal.html))

## Major regional Events

*   **Russian General Shot and Wounded in Moscow**
    *   Lt Gen Vladimir Alexeyev, second-in-command of Russia's GRU military intelligence, was shot and seriously wounded in Moscow.
    *   Russian officials attributed the attack to Ukraine, though no group has claimed responsibility.
    *   Alexeyev played a key role in Ukraine war talks and was previously sanctioned over the Salisbury nerve agent attack.
    *   Impact: Raises concerns about internal security in Russia and potential for further targeted actions against high-ranking military figures.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3686nzexp3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3686nzexp3o))

*   **Deadly Mosque Bombing in Pakistan**
    *   A suicide bombing at a Shia mosque in Islamabad, Pakistan, killed at least 31 people and injured 169 during Friday prayers.
    *   The attack, preceded by gunfire, was condemned by Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif.
    *   Pakistan's Defence Minister linked the attack to India and Afghanistan, which both countries denied.
    *   Impact: Heightens sectarian tensions and underscores persistent security challenges from militancy in Pakistan.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cg7y7gz2835o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cg7y7gz2835o))

*   **Jihadist Attack Kills Dozens in Nigeria**
    *   Over 75 people were killed in a jihadist attack on Muslim villages in Kwara state, Nigeria.
    *   Militants, believed to be a faction of Boko Haram, sought to impose extremist Islamic ideology after villagers refused their preaching.
    *   The attack involved the targeting of local vigilantes and the abduction of 38 women and children.
    *   Impact: Deepens the humanitarian and security crisis in Nigeria, highlighting ongoing vulnerability to extremist violence despite prior warnings.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1dkd3llw1lo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1dkd3llw1lo))

*   **Nigerian Court Orders UK to Pay £420m for Colonial-Era Killings**
    *   A Nigerian court ordered the UK government to pay £420 million ($567 million) to the families of 21 coal miners killed in 1949.
    *   Colonial police shot striking workers in Enugu; the UK government was not represented and denies formal notification.
    *   Impact: A significant ruling for historical accountability and potential precedent for reparations for colonial-era injustices.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g3g51klyno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g3g51klyno))

*   **Humanitarian Crisis Persists in Gaza Hospitals**

    *   Impact: Highlights the severe and ongoing health and humanitarian crisis in Gaza.
