## Executive summary

*   The US-Israel conflict with Iran escalates into its second week, with widespread strikes, civilian casualties, and significant disruptions in the Middle East.
*   Oil prices surged dramatically due to the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, impacting global energy markets and supply chains.
*   Internet blackouts and AI-generated misinformation are prominent features of the Iran conflict, raising concerns about information warfare.
*   US diplomatic efforts focus on countering China's influence in Latin America, while also re-establishing ties with Venezuela after Maduro's capture.
*   Severe weather events in the US and heavy flooding in Kenya caused multiple fatalities and significant damage.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Prices Surge as Strait of Hormuz Disrupted by Iran War.**
    *   US crude oil prices surged 35% this week, the biggest gain in futures trading history.
    *   Tanker traffic in the Strait of Hormuz is at a near standstill due to Iranian threats.
    *   Kuwait cut oil production, and Iraq reduced output, due to lack of storage space.
    *   US announced a $20 billion reinsurance program for oil tankers, but analysts are skeptical it will immediately resolve security concerns.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/iran-us-war-oil-prices-brent-wti-barrel-futures.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/iran-us-war-oil-prices-brent-wti-barrel-futures.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/07/kuwait-oil-cut-iran-war-strait-hormuz.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/07/kuwait-oil-cut-iran-war-strait-hormuz.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/trump-reinsurance-oil-iran-war.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/trump-reinsurance-oil-iran-war.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/trump-navy-strait-hormuz-iran-oil-tanker.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/trump-navy-strait-hormuz-iran-oil-tanker.html))
