## Executive summary

*   **Middle East Conflict Intensifies:** US-Israel airstrikes continue in Iran, causing civilian casualties and "black rain" from oil depot fires; Iran is reportedly mining the Strait of Hormuz, prompting US forces to sink Iranian minelayers.

## Major Regional Events

*   **Middle East Conflict: Iran & Shipping**
    *   US-Israeli airstrikes continue to target Iranian cities, causing power cuts and "black rain" from damaged oil facilities.
        *   Detail: Residents in Tehran and Karaj report exhaustion and fear, with calls for regime change.
        *   Impact: Severe air pollution from burning oil poses health risks; internet connectivity remains heavily restricted.
    *   Iran is reportedly mining the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil shipping route.
        *   Detail: US Central Command reports sinking 16 Iranian minelayers; US Navy is refusing commercial ship escort requests due to high risks.
        *   Impact: Shipping traffic has largely halted, raising global energy prices and exacerbating supply chain disruptions.
    *   Three cargo ships were struck by suspected projectiles off Iran's coast, including one in the Strait of Hormuz.
        *   Detail: One Thai-flagged vessel, the Mayuree Naree, caught fire, forcing crew evacuation.
        *   Context: Iran's Revolutionary Guards claimed responsibility for hitting a vessel in the waterway.
    *   Iran continues to export crude oil to China through the Strait of Hormuz, with at least 11.7 million barrels shipped since February 28.
        *   Context: Many Iranian vessels "go dark" to avoid detection; China is reportedly stockpiling oil.
    *   An Israeli strike hit a luxury hotel in central Beirut, killing four Iranians identified by Iran as diplomats.
        *   Detail: The IDF identified them as senior Quds Force operatives, including a financier for Hezbollah.
        *   Impact: War expands to central Beirut; sparks fear among locals and displaced residents.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cev7llekwllo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cev7llekwllo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqxd1nv3re2o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cqxd1nv3re2o), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/cargo-ship-struck-strait-of-hormuz-uk-iran-war.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/cargo-ship-struck-strait-of-hormuz-uk-iran-war.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/us-strikes-iran-mine-ships-strait-of-hormuz-war-oil.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/us-strikes-iran-mine-ships-strait-of-hormuz-war-oil.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/iran-ships-oil-china-strait-hormuz-closure-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/iran-ships-oil-china-strait-hormuz-closure-.html), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k8y7vy78no](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k8y7vy78no))

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Prices & Emergency Reserves**
    *   The International Energy Agency (IEA) has agreed to release a record 400 million barrels of oil from emergency reserves.
    *   Context: This is a response to supply disruptions and surging prices caused by the US-Israel war with Iran, particularly the near shutdown of the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Impact: Expected to be a short-term solution, as energy analysts caution it may not fully offset the supply disruption, and the global gas market remains challenging.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly093xxlzzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly093xxlzzo), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/iea-oil-reserves-crude-prices-iran-g7-energy.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/iea-oil-reserves-crude-prices-iran-g7-energy.html))

*   **Inflation Concerns & US Economy**
    *   US consumer prices rose 2.4% annually in February, meeting expectations, with core CPI at 2.5%.
    *   Impact: While inflation is stable for now, recent oil price surges from the Iran war are expected to push headline inflation higher in upcoming months.
    *   Context: This could influence the Federal Reserve's interest rate decisions and poses political risk for President Trump ahead of midterm elections.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/cpi-inflation-report-february-2026.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/cpi-inflation-report-february-2026.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/treasury-yields-gain-after-sticky-february-inflation-reading-oil-price-increase.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/treasury-yields-gain-after-sticky-february-inflation-reading-oil-price-increase.html))

*   **Fertilizer Supply Chain Disruption**
    *   The Iran conflict is disrupting global fertilizer shipments through the Strait of Hormuz, impacting the spring planting season.
    *   Detail: Over one-third of global fertilizer trade typically uses this route; urea import prices in the US jumped 30% in a week.
    *   Impact: Could lead to higher global food prices and significant challenges for farmers, retailers, and consumers.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/food-prices-could-rise-as-iran-conflict-disrupts-fertilizer-supply-chain.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/food-prices-could-rise-as-iran-conflict-disrupts-fertilizer-supply-chain.html))

*   **US Oil Refinery Development**
    *   President Trump announced plans for the first new US oil refinery in 50 years, a $300 billion project backed by India's Reliance Industries.
    *   Detail: The refinery in Brownsville, Texas, will process US shale oil and aims to strengthen domestic energy production.
    *   Impact: This initiative is positioned to benefit from elevated global oil prices.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/trump-us-oil-refinery-reliance-ambani-texas-india-shale-wti.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/trump-us-oil-refinery-reliance-ambani-texas-shale-wti.html))

*   **Defense Industry Growth**
    *   German arms manufacturer Rheinmetall projects up to 45% sales growth in 2026, positioning itself to arm the US amid global conflicts.
    *   Context: Increased defense spending due to wars in Ukraine and Iran fuels demand for military capabilities.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/rheinmetall-rhm-fy-earnings-stock-2025.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/rheinmetall-rhm-fy-earnings-stock-2025.html))

*   **Indian Restaurants Face LPG Crisis**
    *   India's restaurants and hotels face a "crisis situation" and potential closures due to a sharp supply crunch in liquefied petroleum gas (LPG).
    *   Context: India's government prioritized household LPG supply over commercial use due to disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Impact: 90% of Indian restaurants rely on LPG; industry seeks "essential service" classification to avert job losses.
