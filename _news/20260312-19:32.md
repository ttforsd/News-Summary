## Executive summary

*   **Iran-US Conflict Escalates:** Iran's new Supreme Leader vows to keep the Strait of Hormuz closed and threatens US military bases, while the US Navy plans to escort vessels. Doubts grow among Iranians who initially supported the strikes.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **Iran-US Tensions Intensify**
    *   Iran's new Supreme Leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, vowed to keep the Strait of Hormuz closed.
    *   He also demanded the immediate closure of all US military bases in the Middle East, threatening attacks.
    *   Khamenei, appointed after his father's assassination, is seen as more hardline.
    *   US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced the US Navy will escort vessels through the Strait of Hormuz when "militarily possible."
    *   US Energy Secretary Chris Wright noted the US is currently focused on destroying Iran's offensive capabilities.
    *   Democratic senators are questioning Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth on a strike that Iranian officials say killed 168 people, including 110 children, at an Iranian primary school.
    *   US military investigators reportedly believe American forces likely caused the unintentional hit, possibly due to outdated intelligence.
    *   The incident, if confirmed, would be one of the worst US civilian casualty cases in decades.
    *   Iran has increased retaliatory attacks, with three more ships struck in the Persian Gulf overnight.
    *   Iranian citizens who initially supported the strikes, hoping for regime change, are growing frustrated due to civilian suffering and increased government oppression.
    *   UNHCR estimates up to 3.2 million Iranians are temporarily displaced by the conflict.

*   **China's Cultural Assimilation Policy**
    *   China approved a new "ethnic unity" law mandating Mandarin teaching from pre-kindergarten to high school.
    *   Critics argue this erodes minority language and cultural rights in regions like Tibet and Xinjiang.
    *   Beijing states the law promotes job prospects and national unity.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp9meeek051o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp9meeek051o))

*   **US Launches Extensive Trade Probes**
    *   The US initiated Section 301 trade investigations into 16 trading partners, including China, the EU, Mexico, Japan, and South Korea.
    *   The probes aim to address "structural excess capacity and production" and "unfair trading practices."
    *   This move follows a Supreme Court ruling against previous Trump administration tariffs, indicating a pivot to new leverage.
    *   (Sources: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/why-section-301-probes-matter-and-what-they-mean-for-trumps-tariffs.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/why-section-301-probes-matter-and-what-they-mean-for-trumps-tariffs.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/trump-raises-the-stakes-on-china-with-section-301-trade-probe-weeks-before-beijing-summit.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/trump-raises-the-stakes-on-china-with-section-301-trade-probe-weeks-before-beijing-summit.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/trump-trade-investigations-ieepa-tariffs.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/trump-trade-investigations-ieepa-tariffs.html))

*   **India-China Economic Re-alignment**
    *   India relaxed investment rules, allowing limited Chinese investments in sectors like electronic components and solar cells.
    *   Minority investments (up to 10% stake) will be expedited, processed within 60 days without government approval.
    *   This signifies a pragmatic recalibration of ties after six years of friction.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/india-china-investments-fdi-ties-business.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/india-china-investments-fdi-ties-business.html))

*   **Senegal Toughens Anti-Gay Law**

    *   UN human rights chief urged the president not to sign, citing concerns about human rights, discrimination, and violence.
    *   Rights groups warn it could harm HIV control efforts and stigmatize LGBTQ+ individuals.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Prices Surge Despite Record Reserve Release**
    *   Oil prices jumped to over $100 a barrel, with Brent crude briefly hitting $100, signaling market skepticism over the impact of an emergency release.
    *   The International Energy Agency (IEA) members agreed to release a record 400 million barrels from strategic reserves to address supply disruption from the Iran war.
    *   The US will contribute 172 million barrels from its Strategic Petroleum Reserve.
    *   The record release indicates energy markets are preparing for a prolonged Middle East conflict.
    *   Analysts predict prices could surge to $120-$150/barrel if the conflict continues for months.
    *   (Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly093xxlzzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cly093xxlzzo), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/plans-for-record-emergency-oil-release-signal-middle-east-war-could-drag-on-for-months.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/plans-for-record-emergency-oil-release-signal-middle-east-war-could-drag-on-for-months.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/cnbc-daily-open-oil-market-crude-prices-reserve-release-iea-us-trump.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/cnbc-daily-open-oil-market-crude-prices-reserve-release-iea-us-trump.html))

*   **Global Reliance on Gulf Energy Exposed**
    *   The US-Israel war with Iran highlights the world's dependence on Gulf oil and gas, with oil prices up over a third since the conflict began.
    *   Asia is highly exposed, receiving nearly 90% of its Strait of Hormuz shipments; South East Asian refineries are specialized for Middle Eastern crude.
    *   The Philippines, Vietnam, and Bangladesh face fuel shortages and price hikes, with governments implementing energy-saving measures.
    *   Saudi Arabia's East-West pipeline (7M bpd capacity) and UAE's ADCOP (1.5-1.8M bpd capacity) are critical bypass routes for the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   (Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c15x1y8d37vo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c15x1y8d37vo), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/the-two-oil-pipelines-helping-saudi-arabia-and-uae-bypass-the-strait-of-hormuz.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/the-two-oil-pipelines-helping-saudi-arabia-and-uae-bypass-the-strait-of-hormuz.html))

*   **Looming Global Food Price Shock**
    *   Disruptions to the Strait of Hormuz, a key artery for fertilizers, threaten a global food price shock.
    *   Higher energy and input costs could lead to reduced crop yields and more expensive food globally.
    *   Gulf countries are immediately exposed, but Sub-Saharan Africa is most vulnerable due to reliance on imported fertilizers for staple crops like maize.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/iran-war-food-prices-fertilizer-hormuz-countries-impacted-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/iran-war-food-prices-fertilizer-hormuz-countries-impacted-.html))

*   **European Energy Prices & Inflation**
    *   The Iran crisis reignited fears of an energy supply squeeze and inflation in Europe, but analysts believe a 2022-style shock can be avoided due to diversification.
    *   Europe has diversified from Russian gas to LNG from various global sources, including the US and Norway.
    *   Prolonged disruptions could still push eurozone inflation to 2.5% and delay central bank rate cuts.
    *   Denmark urged citizens to reduce energy consumption and avoid driving due to spiking oil prices.
    *   (Sources: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/the-iran-war-is-pushing-up-european-energy-prices-heres-why-a-ukraine-style-inflation-shock-could-still-be-avoided.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/the-iran-war-is-pushing-up-european-energy-prices-heres-why-a-ukraine-style-inflation-shock-could-still-be-avoided.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/us-iran-war-danes-energy-driving-oil-prices-spike.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/us-iran-war-danes-energy-driving-oil-prices-spike.html))

*   **European Defense Industry Booms**
    *   European defense firms are experiencing strong growth, with Italy's Leonardo planning to double profits by 2030 and Germany's Rheinmetall forecasting 40-45% sales growth in 2026.
    *   Increased demand is driven by the Iran and Ukraine wars, impacting defense electronics, land systems, and military equipment.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/defense-stocks-leonardo-rheinmetall-stock-rhm-ldo-iran-war-ukraine.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/these-charts-show-the-size-of-europes-defense-boom-as-companies-take-stock-of-the-iran-war.html))

*   **India's Inflation Rises Amid Oil Risks**
    *   India's consumer inflation rose to 3.21% in February, driven by a 3.47% increase in food inflation.
    *   The Iran war threatens India's crude oil (30% of supply) and LPG (90% of imports) from the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   This energy risk is expected to prompt the Reserve Bank of India to maintain current interest rates.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/india-february-inflation-cpi-oil-hormuz-risk.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/india-february-inflation-cpi-oil-hormuz-risk.html))

*   **South Korea's US Investment Pledge**
    *   South Korea's parliament passed a bill to establish a state-run investment corporation to manage a $350 billion investment pledge to the US.
    *   This investment aims to secure favorable tariff rates and includes $150 billion for shipbuilding and $200 billion for strategic sectors.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/south-korea-350-billion-investment-special-bill-us-trump.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/south-korea-350-billion-investment-special-bill-us-trump.html))

*   **Gold Prices Remain Stable**
    *   Gold prices have remained largely unmoved despite the escalating Iran conflict, trading between $5,050 and $5,200 per troy ounce.
    *   Factors like a stronger dollar, higher Treasury yields, and fears of prolonged inflation have deterred investors from a "safe haven" rally.
    *   Long-term forecasts from banks remain bullish, predicting prices of $6,000-$6,300 by end of 2026.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/gold-iran-conflict-where-next-markets.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/gold-iran-conflict-where-next-markets.html))

*   **European Stocks Decline**
    *   European stocks ended lower, with the Stoxx 600 down 0.7%, driven by volatile oil prices and concerns over banks' exposure to the Middle East.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/european-markets-stoxx-600-ftse-dax-cac-iran-news-oil-prices.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/european-markets-stoxx-600-ftse-dax-cac-iran-news-oil-prices.html))

*   **India's Trade Route to Europe**
    *   The Iran war is forcing India to re-evaluate its trade routes to Europe.
    *   The India–Middle East–Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC) is gaining momentum over the International North–South Transport Corridor (INSTC) via Iran, which is stalled due to sanctions and conflict.
    *   IMEC could reduce logistical costs by 30% and transportation time by 40%.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/us-israel-iran-india-trade-europe.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/us-israel-iran-india-trade-europe.html))

    *   The probe, part of a mission studying Earth's radiation belts, discovered a transient third belt.
    *   Most of the spacecraft was expected to burn up, with a low risk of harm on Earth.
