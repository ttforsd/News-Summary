## Executive summary

*   **Middle East Tensions Escalate:** The US is significantly increasing its military presence in the Middle East, while Iran's new Supreme Leader has vowed to maintain the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, intensifying concerns over oil prices and regional stability.
*   **US Sanctions Shift:** The US has temporarily eased sanctions on Russian oil, a move criticized by European allies and Ukraine, who fear it will bolster Russia's war economy.
*   **Economic Headwinds:** Global markets are reacting to prolonged Middle East conflict fears, with European stocks down and UK economic growth stalling. US GDP was revised lower, and inflation remains a concern.
*   **Tech Sector Challenges:** The AI boom faces scrutiny over data center electricity costs and potential supply chain disruptions from the Iran conflict. Elon Musk's xAI is also undergoing a "rebuilding" phase.
*   **Regional Political Change:** Nepal's election saw a new, youth-backed party win a landslide, signaling a desire for change. France is preparing for polarized municipal elections.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US Military Reinforcements in Middle East**
    *   The US is deploying more Marines and warships, including an amphibious ready group with ~5,000 personnel.
    *   This move, and the relocation of a missile defense system from South Korea, aims to bolster US presence.
    *   President Trump has vowed "very hard" action against Iran, with Defense Secretary Hegseth promising "no mercy."

    *   The US authorized a 30-day waiver for purchasing Russian oil already stranded at sea to stabilize energy markets.
