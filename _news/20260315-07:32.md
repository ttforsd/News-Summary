## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Prices Soar Despite Record Emergency Stockpile Release**
    *   Brent crude closed above $100 per barrel for the second consecutive day, surging over 17% since the IEA announcement.
    *   The International Energy Agency (IEA) approved the largest-ever release of 400 million barrels from strategic reserves.
    *   Analysts note the release is likely insufficient to cover the estimated 9 million barrels per day supply gap from the Strait of Hormuz closure.
    *   Saudi Arabia and UAE are increasingly using alternative pipelines to bypass the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/14/the-biggest-release-of-emergency-oil-stockpiles-in-history-was-announced.-why-crude-may-keep-rising.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/14/the-biggest-release-of-emergency-oil-stockpiles-in-history-was-announced.-why-crude-may-keep-rising.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/oil-100-price-brent-wti-trump-iran-war-surrender-khamenei.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/oil-100-price-brent-wti-trump-iran-war-surrender-khamenei.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/the-two-oil-pipelines-helping-saudi-arabia-and-uae-bypass-the-strait-of-hormuz.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/strait-of-hormuz-oil-pipelines-iran-war-saudi-arabia-uae.html))
