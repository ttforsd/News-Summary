## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Energy Markets & Supply Chains:**
    *   Global oil prices remain volatile, with Brent crude closing above $100/barrel for the second consecutive day.
      *   The market largely shrugged off the IEA's record 400 million barrels emergency oil release.
      *   Analysts warn the release is insufficient to cover the supply gap caused by the Strait of Hormuz closure. (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/oil-100-price-brent-wti-trump-iran-war-surrender-khamenei.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/oil-100-price-brent-wti-trump-iran-war-surrender-khamenei.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/14/iran-war-iea-oil-stockpile-spr-strait-hormuz.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/14/iran-war-iea-oil-stockpile-spr-strait-hormuz.html))
    *   Saudi Arabia's East-West pipeline and UAE's Abu Dhabi Crude Oil Pipeline (ADCOP) offer alternative routes to bypass the Strait of Hormuz.
      *   These pipelines have a combined capacity of 8.5-8.8 million barrels per day, but are vulnerable to infrastructure damage. (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/strait-of-hormuz-oil-pipelines-iran-war-saudi-arabia-uae.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/strait-of-hormuz-oil-pipelines-iran-war-saudi-arabia-uae.html))
    *   Countries are implementing various measures to cope with the Iran war-driven energy shock.
      *   China ordered refiners to halt fuel exports; Japan and South Korea are considering price caps.
      *   Thailand encouraged civil servants to take stairs and wear short-sleeved shirts to conserve energy. (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/15/price-caps-and-stairs-how-nations-are-coping-with-the-iran-oil-shock.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/15/price-caps-and-stairs-how-nations-are-coping-with-the-iran-oil-shock.html))

      *   Sovereign bond markets have seen a sell-off, indicating growing concerns over inflation and stagflation risks.
