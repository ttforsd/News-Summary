## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Iran War's Economic Fallout**
    *   The Iran war has severely disrupted global energy markets, sending oil prices surging by over 40% since late February, with Dubai crude reaching $166 a barrel.
    *   Daily shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz is down 95%, with risks from drones, missiles, and mines.
    *   Some countries like Norway, Canada, and Russia are benefiting from higher oil and gas prices.
    *   Conversely, oil-importing nations like the US, UK, and Europe face increased inflation risk.
    *   India is experiencing gas shortages, threatening a shift back to polluting fuels and impacting its busiest IPO market, which is seeing deferred listings and foreign investor outflows.
    *   The US faces a fertilizer shortage and price hikes due to the Strait's closure, impacting farm states and potentially raising food prices, becoming a political issue ahead of midterms.

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

    *   The Trump administration unveiled a legislative framework for a national AI policy to establish uniform safety and security guardrails.
    *   The framework aims to preempt individual states from enacting their own AI regulations, which the administration argues would hinder innovation.

*   **AI in Commerce and Future of Work**
    *   OpenAI is shifting its online shopping strategy from "Instant Checkout" to dedicated retailer apps within ChatGPT. This change aims to give retailers more control and improve product discovery.
    *   Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang proposed offering "AI tokens" in addition to salaries for engineers, empowering them to deploy AI agents to boost productivity. He foresees hundreds of thousands of AI agents working at Nvidia.
