## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Market Volatility and Supply Disruptions**
    *   Oil prices surpassed $112/barrel after Iraq declared force majeure and Kuwaiti refineries were attacked.
        *   Iraq cited inability to ship crude through the disrupted Strait of Hormuz.
        *   Strait of Hormuz traffic is down 95% since early March due to conflict.
        *   Saudi officials project oil could reach $180 if disruptions persist through April.
        *   The US temporarily lifted sanctions on some Iranian oil to ease global prices.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/20/oil-wti-brent-us-weighs-releasing-sanctioned-iranian-crude.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/20/oil-wti-brent-us-weighs-releasing-sanctioned-iranian-crude.html), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9d415g55nno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9d415g55nno), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4geg0eeyjeo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4geg0eeyjeo))

*   **Inflation Fears Drive Bond Market Shifts**
    *   Global bond markets are experiencing a sell-off and rising yields due to Iran war-induced inflation fears.
        *   US 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.39%; 2-year yield also climbed.
        *   European sovereign bonds face a "perfect storm," with UK Gilts hitting new highs.
        *   Central banks (Fed, ECB, BoE) held rates, but markets now price in future hikes.
        *   Analysts suggest up to three ECB rate hikes this year to contain prices.
        *   Japan faces "cost-push" inflation from higher energy costs and a weak yen.
