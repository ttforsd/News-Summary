        *   This strong performance may lead the Federal Reserve to delay interest rate cuts.
        *   Economic uncertainty persists due to the Iran war's potential impact on energy prices. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crk165g4vv3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crk165g4vv3o))

*   **UK Domestic Economic & Social Policy:**
    *   A host of UK benefits and the state pension are increasing as the new financial year begins.
        *   The nine-year-old two-child benefit cap has been scrapped, benefiting 480,000 families with an average £4,100 annual rise.
        *   State pension increases by 4.8%; Universal Credit basic allowance rises by £120.
        *   The health element of Universal Credit is halved for new claimants, though existing claimants are protected.
        *   Income tax thresholds remain frozen, increasing revenue through "stealth tax." (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn085pdly86o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn085pdly86o))
    *   New UK laws, effective by spring 2027, will make it easier for consumers to cancel subscriptions and get refunds.
        *   Firms must provide clear information upfront, send renewal reminders, and offer a 14-day cooling-off period.
        *   Aims to save consumers an estimated £400m annually by combating "subscription traps." (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvg0v36ek2go](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvg0v36ek2go))

*   **European Agriculture:**

        *   Pork prices have fallen sharply, with over €600m in losses, and several countries have halted imports.
        *   Catalonia is culling wild boar to control the highly contagious virus, which does not affect humans. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2ev3p14kvlo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2ev3p14kvlo))
