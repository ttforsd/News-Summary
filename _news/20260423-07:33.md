## Executive summary

*   **Middle East tensions intensify:** Iran seized two container ships in the Strait of Hormuz, rejecting US peace talks, while Lebanon accused Israel of war crimes after a journalist was killed in an air strike. The US Navy Secretary also resigned amidst the ongoing conflict.
*   **Ukraine receives EU support:** The EU approved a €90bn loan and sanctions package for Ukraine after Russian oil flow resumed through the Druzhba pipeline to Hungary and Slovakia, resolving a deadlock.
*   **Global economic impacts of Iran war:** Soaring oil prices, partly due to Hormuz disruptions, prompted Lufthansa to cut 20,000 flights and led Germany to halve its economic growth forecast. China's manufacturing also faces higher costs and reduced orders.
*   **AI investments and developments:** Microsoft announced an $18bn AI investment in Australia. SK Hynix reported record profits driven by AI memory demand. Meta plans to track employee activity for AI training, raising privacy concerns.
*   **International institutions questioned:** Canada's former PM Justin Trudeau voiced concerns that global organizations are "ill-adjusted" to modern challenges, advocating for "microlateralism."
