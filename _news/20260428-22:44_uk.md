## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **UAE exits Opec:** The United Arab Emirates will formally exit the Opec and Opec+ groups of oil-producing nations next month.
    *   This decision, after nearly 60 years of membership, is driven by the UAE's long-term goal to boost its oil production capacity.
    *   Impact: Analysts believe this could mark "the beginning of the end" for Opec's influence, potentially leading to lower oil prices and increased market volatility globally.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4pxwlr52yo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4pxwlr52yo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4pxyklw1jo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4pxyklw1jo))
*   **BP profits surge amid Iran war:** BP reported first-quarter profits of $3.2bn, more than double the previous year, surpassing analyst expectations.
    *   The surge is attributed to an "exceptional" performance in its oil trading division, benefiting from significant oil price swings caused by the Iran war and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Impact: Highlights how geopolitical conflicts can fuel substantial earnings for energy giants, intensifying calls for stronger windfall taxes.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2eveyvgn9no](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2eveyvgn9no))
*   **UK demographic shifts predicted:** The Office for National Statistics projects more deaths than births in the UK annually from 2026 onwards.
    *   The UK population is expected to grow at a slower rate, reaching 71 million by 2034, due to declining fertility rates and a sharp fall in net migration.
    *   Impact: This demographic shift will place increased pressure on public finances, including pensions, health, and social care systems.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgpjd2zzl8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgpjd2zzl8o))

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Musk's OpenAI lawsuit begins:** Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI and its co-founders has officially opened in California federal court.
    *   Musk alleges that OpenAI has deviated from its original non-profit mission of benefiting humanity, accusing them of "charity theft" for commercial gain.
    *   OpenAI counters that Musk's suit is motivated by rivalry and regret over his failed attempts to control the company.
    *   Impact: The trial's outcome could set precedents for the governance and commercialization of artificial intelligence development.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz027nyz529o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz027nyz529o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn8dedv8w8xo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn8dedv8w8xo))
*   **Bird flu vaccine trial launched:** The UK has initiated a clinical trial for an H5N1 bird flu vaccine, utilizing mRNA technology.
    *   The trial aims to assess the vaccine's safety and effectiveness in generating a strong immune response against a potential pandemic strain.
    *   Impact: This proactive measure is a significant step in bolstering global pandemic preparedness, leveraging lessons from COVID-19 vaccine development.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy41z0yj8mjo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy41z0yj8mjo))
*   **RSV pregnancy vaccine success:** A vaccine administered during pregnancy is proving highly effective, reducing hospital admissions for babies with RSV by over 80% in the UK.
    *   The vaccine works by passing maternal antibodies to newborns, providing "excellent protection" against severe respiratory syncytial virus infections.
    *   Impact: Represents a major public health advancement, significantly reducing a leading cause of infant hospitalization and associated healthcare burdens.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g84nxwz8wo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g84nxwz8wo))
*   **Palantir defends NHS data contracts:** The data analytics firm Palantir is defending its £300m NHS England contracts amidst calls for increased scrutiny from UK MPs.
    *   Concerns have been raised regarding data privacy, national security implications, and the company's "values" due to its historical funding ties (e.g., CIA) and international contracts.
    *   Impact: Fuels the ongoing debate about the role of private technology companies in critical public sector infrastructure, especially concerning sensitive patient data.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c393w38lv3mo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c393w38lv3mo))
*   **AI chatbot health advice risks:** New research highlights the dangers of relying on AI chatbots for health advice, finding them to be "confident and wrong" in two-thirds of real-world scenarios.
    *   Patients seeking personalized advice could receive inaccurate diagnoses or inappropriate care recommendations.
    *   Impact: Raises critical public safety concerns and emphasizes the need for caution and expert oversight in the application of AI in healthcare.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyepyy82kxo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyepyy82kxo))
