## Executive Summary

*   **Middle East tensions remain high:** Deadly Israeli strikes continue in southern Lebanon despite a ceasefire; Israel intercepted a Gaza-bound aid flotilla near Crete.
*   **Global oil prices volatile:** Brent crude surged then retreated, influenced by US-Iran war dynamics and ongoing blockade of the Strait of Hormuz.
*   **Central banks maintain rates:** Both the Bank of England and European Central Bank held key interest rates, citing persistent inflation concerns from the Iran war.
*   **US economic mixed signals:** First-quarter GDP growth was lower than expected at 2%, while core inflation rose and jobless claims hit a generational low.
*   **Big Tech drives AI investment:** Apple and Reddit reported strong earnings, and major tech firms project over $1 trillion in AI capital expenditures by 2027.
*   **Myanmar leader under house arrest:** Aung San Suu Kyi moved from prison to house arrest by the military junta.
*   **China boosts African trade:** China removes tariffs for most African nations, excluding Eswatini due to Taiwan ties, as part of a soft power push.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Germany Rift Deepens Amid Iran War:**
    *   President Trump announced the US is "studying" troop reductions in Germany.
    *   This follows German Chancellor Merz's criticism of the US approach to the Iran war.
    *   Trump also criticized Italy and Spain for their lack of support regarding the Iran conflict.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyplg23l30o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyplg23l30o), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/us-reviews-cutting-troop-numbers-in-germany-amid-trumps-berlin-feud-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/us-reviews-cutting-troop-numbers-in-germany-amid-trumps-berlin-feud-.html))

    *   The former leader, 80, was moved from military prison to house arrest.
    *   She has been detained since the 2021 military coup; her son is skeptical of the announcement.
    *   Impact: Seen by some as a PR move by the junta seeking to ease international isolation.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz72j8eex4eo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cz72j8eex4eo))

    *   China will scrap tariffs for 53 African countries until April 2028 to enhance soft power.
    *   Eswatini is excluded due to its diplomatic ties with Taiwan, highlighting political leverage.
    *   Impact: Aims to boost agricultural exports and rural incomes in Africa, but trade imbalance persists.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2v509217o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2v509217o))
*   **Japan Urges Greater Regional Defense Burden-Sharing:**
    *   Former Defense Minister Taro Kono stated Japan relies too heavily on the US for security.
    *   He called for Asian countries to shoulder more defense responsibility amid rising China tensions.
    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/japan-us-security-burden-taro-kono-defense-china-tensions-taro-kono.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/japan-us-security-burden-taro-kono-defense-china-tensions-taro-kono.html))
*   **Venice Biennale Jury Resigns Over Geopolitical Stance:**
    *   The entire jury stepped down due to tensions over Russia and Israel's participation.
    *   The jury cited a policy not to award prizes to countries whose leaders face ICC charges.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c78qdg4g80eo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c78qdg4g80eo))
