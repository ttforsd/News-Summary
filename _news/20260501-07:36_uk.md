    *   Fertiliser prices have soared by 80% since the conflict began.
    *   This could lead to a global food bidding war, disproportionately affecting poorer nations. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpwp50v4ye7o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpwp50v4ye7o))

    *   Bank of England warned of potential interest rate hikes to curb inflation fueled by energy shock.
    *   UK homeowners face an average £80 monthly mortgage payment rise. ## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Medication Shortages:** England faces worsening shortages of hundreds of everyday drugs.
    *   Patients with chronic conditions like epilepsy and Parkinson's are severely impacted.
    *   Problem stems from surging global prices and the UK's slow drug reimbursement system.
    *   Pharmacies are dispensing at a loss, leading to closures; 1,500 High Street pharmacies have shut since 2017. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c202jqn3jzro](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c202jqn3jzro))
*   **Cancer in Young People:** Major analysis reveals 11 cancers are increasing in young people in England.
    *   Overweight and obesity are identified as a likely contributing factor, though not the sole cause.
    *   Bowel and breast cancers are the most common types seen rising in younger adults. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crepdjdj0z4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crepdjdj0z4o))
*   **Healthy Life Expectancy Decline:** UK healthy life expectancy has fallen by two years in the past decade.
    *   Now below 61 for both men and women, placing the UK 20th out of 21 rich countries.
    *   Linked to poverty, poor housing, obesity, mental ill health, and the impact of Covid. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c20q07w3gl9o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c20q07w3gl9o))
*   **Miscarriage Care Improvement:** A pilot scheme for early miscarriage care in England shows promise.
    *   Offers earlier checks and advice, potentially preventing thousands of miscarriages annually.
    *   Challenges the NHS guideline of waiting for three miscarriages before specialist help. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62r8lzgnk2o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62r8lzgnk2o))
*   **Smoking Ban Approved:** UK Parliament passed a law banning smoking for anyone born after January 1, 2009.
    *   Ministers gain new powers to regulate tobacco, vaping products, flavors, and packaging.
    *   Vaping will be prohibited in cars with children, playgrounds, and near schools/hospitals. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn08jy6w0l5o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn08jy6w0l5o))
*   **Bird Flu Vaccine Trial:** First volunteers in the UK received a vaccine targeting potential H5N1 bird flu pandemic strain.
    *   Uses mRNA technology for rapid production in a pandemic scenario.
    *   Trial aims to recruit poultry workers and those over 65, the most at-risk groups. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy41z0yj8mjo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy41z0yj8mjo))
*   **Healthcare Workforce Strain:** Health visitors in England report "impossible" caseloads, with some managing over 1,000 families.
    *   Number of health visitors has almost halved in the last decade.
    *   Calls for safe staffing limits to prevent "human cost" of prioritizing families. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8lp0pz3y9o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8lp0pz3y9o))
