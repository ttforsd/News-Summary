## Executive summary

*   **UK Elections:** Labour suffered significant losses in local and national elections across England, Wales, and Scotland, increasing pressure on Sir Keir Starmer's leadership. Reform UK made substantial gains.
*   **Middle East Tensions:** Iran accused the US of "reckless military adventure" after new clashes in the Strait of Hormuz, with the US disabling Iranian oil tankers. Oil prices surged.
*   **Japan's Defence Shift:** Large anti-war protests erupted in Japan as the Prime Minister pushes for a stronger defence posture, altering the country's post-war pacifist stance.
*   **Hantavirus Outbreak:** A global effort is underway to trace passengers from a cruise ship affected by a hantavirus outbreak, with five confirmed cases and three deaths; the WHO states the public risk is low.
*   **Tech Privacy Reversal:** Meta has removed end-to-end encryption from Instagram Direct Messages globally, drawing criticism from privacy advocates.

## Major regional Events

*   **UK Election Results Reshape Political Landscape**
    *   Labour suffered heavy losses in English local elections, losing control of over 20 councils.
    *   Impact: Increased pressure on Labour leader Sir Keir Starmer, with calls for a leadership review.
    *   Reform UK achieved significant gains, taking control of several councils in England and its first London borough (Havering).
    *   Wales: Labour faced a historic defeat, losing its long-held dominance in the Senedd; Plaid Cymru emerged as the largest party.
    *   Scotland: The SNP is forecast to remain the largest party in Holyrood, though potentially short of a majority.
