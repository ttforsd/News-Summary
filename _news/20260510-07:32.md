## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Iran War Impact on Energy Markets:**
    *   Oil executives predict the Iran war will cause "fundamental structural change" in global energy systems.
    *   Governments are prioritizing energy security, diversifying supplies, and rebuilding oil stockpiles.
    *   Investments are expected to increase in oil exploration, production (especially offshore), nuclear, and geothermal energy.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/09/iran-war-oil-gas-energy-security.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/09/iran-war-oil-gas-energy-security.html))
*   **Oil Market Prolonged Shock (NACHO Trade):**
    *   Traders are adopting the "NACHO" (Not A Chance Hormuz Opens) trade, skeptical of a quick resolution to the Strait of Hormuz crisis.
    *   Oil and shipping markets signal fears of prolonged disruption, with Brent crude above $100 a barrel.
    *   War premiums for Hormuz transits surged, indicating lasting structural macro risk rather than temporary volatility.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/traders-nacho-trade-strait-hormuz-oil-markets-taco-trump-iran.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/traders-nacho-trade-strait-hormuz-oil-markets-taco-trump-iran.html))
*   **Federal Reserve Interest Rate Outlook:**
    *   The Federal Reserve has fewer reasons to cut interest rates due to a stabilizing labor market and persistent inflation.
    *   April's nonfarm payrolls increase (115,000) reduces pressure for cuts, while March CPI (3.3%) remains above target.
    *   FOMC may remove its easing bias, suggesting a more hawkish posture or rate hikes.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/the-federal-reserve-is-quickly-running-out-of-reasons-to-cut-interest-rates.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/the-federal-reserve-is-quickly-running-out-of-reasons-to-cut-interest-rates.html))
*   **UK Political Impact on Markets:**
    *   UK PM Keir Starmer affirmed he will not resign despite significant local election losses for Labour.
    *   Gilt yields eased slightly, as markets had priced in a worse outcome for Labour.
    *   Starmer appointed former PM Gordon Brown as Special Envoy on Global Finance and Cooperation.
