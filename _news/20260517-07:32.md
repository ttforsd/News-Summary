## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Market Under Pressure as Hormuz Closure Continues**
    *   Global oil stockpiles are falling at a record pace due to Iran's blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, nearing critical levels by the end of May.
    *   The International Energy Agency warns of impending price spikes and a severe economic contraction if the closure persists.
    *   US and China actions (US increasing exports, China reducing imports) have mitigated a larger price surge, covering about 70% of the supply loss.
    *   The UAE is fast-tracking a second West-East oil pipeline, expected by 2027, to double its export capacity and bypass the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Oil prices jumped after President Trump expressed impatience with Iran, claiming Xi Jinping wants the Strait reopened and agreed to buy more US oil.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/16/oil-inventory-stockpile-iran-war-strait-hormuz.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/16/oil-inventory-stockpile-iran-war-strait-hormuz.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/china-us-oil-iran-war-strait-hormuz-trump-xi.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/china-us-oil-iran-war-strait-hormuz.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/oil-prices-china-us-iran-strait-of-hormuz-middle-east.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/oil-prices-china-us-iran-strait-hormuz-middle-east.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/uae-west-east-oil-pipeline-strait-hormuz-iran-war.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/uae-west-east-oil-pipeline-strait-hormuz-iran-war.html))
*   **UK Markets Wary of Potential Labour Leadership Shift**
    *   The British pound slumped and gilts sold off amid fears of a leftward shift in Labour Party leadership if Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham challenges Keir Starmer.
    *   Investors are concerned about a potential increase in government spending and borrowing under a more left-leaning administration.
    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/andy-burnham-uk-keir-starmer-labour-election-bonds-gilts-sterling-markets.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/andy-burnham-uk-keir-starmer-labour-election-bonds-gilts-sterling-markets.html))
*   **Global Inflation Fears Mount, Driving Market Sell-off**
    *   Government bonds, global equities, and precious metals experienced a sell-off as inflation fears resurfaced.
    *   The US 10-year Treasury yield hit its highest level in nearly a year, and spot silver fell by 7%.
    *   Mounting inflation expectations, geopolitical tensions, and uncertainty around the Iran war contributed to the negative sentiment.
    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/bonds-stocks-and-precious-metals-slump-as-inflation-fears-mount-silver-falls-7.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/bonds-stocks-and-precious-metals-slump-as-inflation-fears-mount-silver-falls-7.html))
*   **Aviation and Tourism Sectors Face Headwinds**
    *   European airlines are cutting flights and increasing surcharges due to the jet fuel crisis caused by the Strait of Hormuz blockade.
    *   Holidaymakers are shifting to closer destinations (e.g., France, Spain) and opting for rail travel or staycations due to increased costs and uncertainty.
    *   Singapore Airlines' investment in Air India is facing significant losses (SG$3.56 billion) compounded by the Iran war's impact on flight connectivity, though SIA remains committed for the "long game."
    *   Goa is seeing a near halving of foreign tourist numbers since 2017, attributed to visa issues, higher costs, and competition from other Asian destinations.
    *   Canada is allowing 49,000 Chinese-made EVs annually with a 6.1% tariff, opening its market despite criticism from US officials.

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **WHO Declares Ebola Outbreak a Global Health Emergency**
    *   The World Health Organization (WHO) has declared an Ebola outbreak in DR Congo's eastern Ituri province a Public Health Emergency of International Concern.
    *   Around 246 suspected cases and 80 deaths have been reported from the Bundibugyo virus strain, for which no approved drugs or vaccines exist.
    *   The virus has spread to neighboring Uganda, raising concerns about regional spread due to population mobility.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2l2p0wwzzdo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2l2p0wwzzdo))
*   **Hantavirus Case Confirmed After Cruise Ship Outbreak**
    *   A Canadian passenger from the MV Hondius cruise ship has tested positive for Hantavirus, bringing total infections to 11 (with 3 deaths).
    *   The Andes strain of Hantavirus may allow human transmission, but officials maintain the risk of a major outbreak is low.
    *   Source: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno))
*   **Pharmaceutical Companies Innovate in Psoriasis and Drug Development**
    *   Johnson & Johnson is launching Icotyde, a new psoriasis pill, with potential to become one of its largest drugs ever, aiming for $10.5 billion in sales by 2032.
    *   Bristol Myers Squibb is partnering with China's Hengrui Pharma for cross-continent drug development, including sending experimental drugs to China for early testing.
    *   This signals a trend of US/European pharma looking to China for faster and cheaper drug development, positioning China as an emerging global R&D hub.
    *   Source: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/16/why-jj-thinks-its-new-psoriasis-pill-could-be-one-of-its-biggest-drugs-ever.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/16/why-jj-thinks-its-new-psoriasis-pill-could-be-one-of-its-biggest-drugs-ever.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/bristol-myers-squibb-turns-to-chinas-hengrui-to-replenish-pipeline.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/bristol-myers-squibb-turns-to-chinas-hengrui-to-replenish-pipeline.html))
*   **Debate on Medically Assisted Dying in Canada**
    *   A 49-year-old Canadian woman with severe mental illness is seeking medically assisted dying (MAID), challenging the current law that restricts access for those whose sole condition is mental illness.
    *   Canada has twice delayed expanding MAID to include mental illness due to ongoing debate over the "incurability" of mental conditions and potential systemic issues in care.
