## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Peace Talks Stalled Amid Trump's Warning**
    *   President Trump issued a stern warning to Iran, stating "the Clock is Ticking" for peace negotiations and threatening severe consequences if Iran fails to act swiftly.

## Major regional Events

*   **Ukraine Launches Large-Scale Drone Attack on Moscow Region**
    *   Ukrainian drones struck the Moscow region, killing at least three people and injuring several.
    *   Russian officials reported a woman died in Khimki and two in Pogorelki; an Indian citizen was also killed.
    *   Ukrainian President Zelensky stated the strikes were an "entirely justified" response to recent Russian attacks on Ukrainian cities.
    *   Russia's military claimed 556 drones were intercepted nationwide, 130 in the Moscow region.
    *   Ukraine's SBU service claimed strikes on oil facilities, a semiconductor plant near Moscow, and air defense systems in Crimea.
    *   Separately, Russian drone attacks in Ukraine's Dnipropetrovsk region injured eight people and damaged houses.
    *   **Sources:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5ye480kxpmo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5ye480kxpmo))

*   **Intensified Warfare on Ukraine's Front Line**
    *   Eastern Ukraine's front lines are characterized as a "kill-zone" due to pervasive drone activity.
    *   Soldier rotations are extremely difficult, and assaults are small-scale, prioritizing speed over armor to evade drones.
    *   Russian advances in Donbas are reportedly slowed, but Moscow aims to capture Kramatorsk and Sloviansk.
    *   Ukraine is increasing attacks on Russian logistics in anticipation of a summer offensive.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3w2xqj9x13o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3w2xqj9x13o))

*   **Middle East Tensions Escalate with Drone Strikes**
    *   The UAE reported a drone strike near its Barakah Nuclear Power Plant in Abu Dhabi, calling it a "dangerous escalation."
    *   One of three incoming drones struck an electrical generator outside the plant; no radiological impact or injuries reported.
    *   The UAE foreign ministry deemed it an "unacceptable act of aggression."
    *   Separately, Saudi Arabia intercepted three drones originating from Iraq.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy27pkj1l1o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy27pkj1l1o))

*   **Hezbollah Intensifies Drone Attacks on Israel**
    *   Hezbollah is increasingly using small First-Person View (FPV) drones, many with fibre-optic cables, to attack Israeli targets.
    *   Experts indicate Israel Defense Forces (IDF) struggle to counter these cheap, hard-to-detect drones.
    *   BBC Verify confirmed 35 FPV drone strikes since March 26, targeting soldiers, vehicles, and air defenses.
    *   This marks an escalation since March 2, following US-Israeli attacks in Iran.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1j2zwe9g5no](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1j2zwe9g5no))

*   **Surge in Political Executions in Iran**
    *   The UN confirmed at least 32 political prisoner executions in Iran since February 28, a significant year-on-year increase.
    *   Amnesty International reported 45 politically motivated executions in all of 2025.
    *   Human rights groups accuse Iran of using the death penalty to suppress dissent and target minorities, citing forced confessions.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn8p392nl7yo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn8p392nl7yo))

*   **Over 50 Children Kidnapped in Northeast Nigeria**
    *   Gunmen abducted more than 50 children, aged two to five, from three schools in Mussa, Borno state.
    *   Eyewitnesses reported the attackers used children as human shields while fleeing on motorbikes.
    *   No group has claimed responsibility; suspected groups include Boko Haram or Islamic State West Africa Province (ISWAP).
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c202vrxlwqgo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c202vrxlwqgo))

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Oil Prices Surge Amid Heightened Middle East Tensions**
    *   Oil prices jumped over 1% after President Trump's warning to Iran, intensifying fears of further conflict escalation and supply disruptions.
    *   Brent crude futures for July rose to $111.42 a barrel; US West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures advanced to $107.98.
    *   The International Energy Agency (IEA) warned that global oil inventories are depleting at a record pace due to the prolonged closure of the Strait of Hormuz, which usually handles nearly a fifth of the world's oil and gas supply.
    *   G7 finance ministers are meeting in Paris to discuss economic consequences of the Strait's closure.
    *   **Sources:** ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/oil-today-brent-wti-iran-trump-hormuz-iea-supply-crude-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/oil-today-brent-wti-iran-trump-hormuz-iea-supply-crude-.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/17/opening-strait-of-hormuz-is-of-utmost-importance-eurogroup-head.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/17/opening-strait-of-hormuz-is-of-utmost-importance-eurogroup-head.html))

*   **China's Economic Growth Slows in April**
    *   China's retail sales grew a mere 0.2% in April, marking a 40-month low and missing economists' forecasts.
    *   Industrial output climbed 4.1%, below expectations, and urban fixed asset investment contracted 1.6% in the first four months.
    *   Property sector investment plunged 13.7%, and crude oil refining volumes fell for a second consecutive month.
    *   Strong exports partially mitigated domestic weaknesses, but energy strains from the Iran war are pushing up commodity and producer prices.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/china-april-retail-sales-industrial-output-investment-unemployment-iran-war.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/china-april-retail-sales-industrial-output-investment-unemployment-iran-war.html))

*   **Asian and European Markets Anticipate Decline**
    *   Asia-Pacific markets mostly fell due to renewed Middle East concerns, with Japan's Nikkei 225 losing 0.87% and Hong Kong's Hang Seng index falling 1.43%.
    *   South Korea's Kospi initially tumbled but reversed losses, though overall market volatility neared record highs after foreign investors sold $13.2 billion in equities.
    *   European stocks are expected to open lower, with major indices like FTSE 100, DAX, CAC 40, and FTSE MIB all projected to decline.
    *   Long-term borrowing costs in G7 economies, including US 30-year Treasuries and UK gilts, have surged on inflation fears.
    *   **Sources:** ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/asia-pacific-markets-today-live-updates-nikkei-225-hang-seng-index-kospi-nifty-50.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/asia-pacific-markets-today-live-updates-nikkei-225-hang-seng-index-kospi-nifty-50.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/s-korea-market-volatility-near-record-as-foreigners-sell-13-billion.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/s-korea-market-volatility-near-record-as-foreigners-sell-13-billion.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/european-markets-stoxx-600-ftse-dax-iran-latest-uk-news.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/european-markets-stoxx-600-ftse-dax-iran-latest-uk-news.html))

*   **Samsung Faces Potential Strike, Impacting South Korean Economy**
    *   An 18-day strike looms at Samsung Electronics starting May 21, with over 47,000 workers potentially participating.
    *   South Korean President Lee Jae Myung urged a labor deal, warning of "significant damage" to the national economy, as Samsung accounts for 12.5% of the country's GDP.
    *   The union demands increased performance bonuses, while management offered a smaller percentage and a one-time package.

*   **UK Lender Collapse Rattles Global Financial Firms**
    *   The insolvency of UK specialist lender Market Financial Solutions (MFS) has exposed major US and European financial institutions to potentially hundreds of millions in losses.
    *   Barclays, HSBC, Jefferies, Wells Fargo, Apollo, and Elliott Management are among firms impacted.
    *   Allegations of fraud, including "double pledging" assets, and a significant collateral shortfall are being investigated.
    *   The incident highlights risks in complex private credit structures and demands greater regulatory scrutiny.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/mfs-private-credit-insolvency-banks-failure-collapse-barclays-mortgage.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/mfs-private-credit-insolvency-banks-failure-collapse-barclays-mortgage.html))

*   **Airline Industry Grapples with Jet Fuel Shortage and Cost**
    *   European airlines face a challenging summer due to a 103% surge in jet fuel prices since March and supply shortages from the Middle East conflict.
    *   Ryanair, while profitable, warned that weaker carriers might not survive the winter.
    *   Consumers are increasingly opting for closer European destinations or staycations and shifting to rail travel to avoid increased airfares and cancellations.
    *   Singapore Airlines (SIA) continues to back Air India despite significant losses linked to regional conflicts and Middle East connectivity.
    *   The UAE announced its exit from OPEC, citing strategic economic vision and national interest, aiming to pursue its own 4.9 million BPD capacity goal and building a pipeline to bypass the Strait of Hormuz.

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Ebola Outbreak Declared International Emergency in DR Congo**
    *   The World Health Organization (WHO) declared the Ebola outbreak in DR Congo's Ituri province a public health emergency of international concern.
    *   Around 246 suspected cases and 80 deaths have been reported in Ituri.
    *   The Bundibugyo virus strain has no approved drugs or vaccines.
    *   Cases are confirmed in the capital Kinshasa and two in neighboring Uganda, raising concerns about regional spread due to conflict and population mobility.
    *   WHO advises enhanced surveillance, isolation of cases, and urges against border closures.
    *   **Sources:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2l2p0wwzzdo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2l2p0wwzzdo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9q311nj5r3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9q311nj5r3o))

*   **Canadian Tests Positive for Hantavirus from Cruise Ship**
    *   A Canadian passenger from the MV Hondius cruise ship tested positive for hantavirus, developing mild symptoms while isolating.
    *   This brings the total confirmed infections to 11, with three deaths linked to the virus.
    *   The WHO recommended a 42-day isolation period for affected individuals.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno))

*   **Novo Nordisk Expands Global Battle for Weight-Loss Drug Market**
    *   Novo Nordisk plans an "all in" global launch of its Wegovy pill outside the US, its primary market.
    *   The drugmaker expects selective international launches based on patient interest, doctor training, and potential telehealth partnerships.
    *   The Wegovy pill has shown impressive uptake in the US, with over 2 million prescriptions.
    *   Eli Lilly's rival pill, Foundayo, has recorded slower initial prescription rates.

*   **J&J's New Psoriasis Pill, Icotyde, Poised for Major Success**
    *   Johnson & Johnson (J&J) is betting on its newly launched psoriasis pill, Icotyde, to become one of its biggest drugs.
    *   Approved by the FDA in mid-March, it's the first oral treatment targeting the IL-23 receptor for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis.
    *   J&J aims for Icotyde and its injectable Tremfya to surpass waning sales of Stelara, with analysts projecting Icotyde sales reaching $10.5 billion by 2032.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/16/why-jj-thinks-its-new-psoriasis-pill-could-be-one-of-its-biggest-drugs-ever.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/16/why-jj-thinks-its-new-psoriasis-pill-could-be-one-of-its-biggest-drugs-ever.html))

*   **Europe's AI Ambitions Jeopardized by High Energy Costs**
    *   Europe's goal to lead in AI is threatened by electricity prices that are roughly double those in the US.
    *   The high energy consumption of data centers makes AI investments particularly sensitive to cost.
    *   Experts suggest data center projects may migrate to regions with cheaper power, favoring Nordic countries and France (due to nuclear energy).
    *   OpenAI reportedly paused its Stargate project in the UK partly due to energy costs and regulatory environment.
    *   **Source:** ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/europe-ai-energy-electricity-costs-data-centers-china-us.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/europe-ai-energy-electricity-costs-data-centers-china-us.html))
