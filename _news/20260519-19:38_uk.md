## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **UK HS2 Rail Project Facing Major Reset:**
    *   The cost of the HS2 high-speed rail project could rise to £102.7bn, with trains not running until 2036-2039.
    *   The top speed will be reduced to 320km/h; current project runs only from London to Birmingham.
    *   Cancellation costs are nearly equivalent to completing the scaled-back first phase.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c794xw7p2dqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c794xw7p2dqo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgzrle9441o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgzrle9441o))
*   **UK Government Urges Supermarkets on Price Caps:**
    *   The UK government is asking supermarkets to voluntarily limit prices on staple goods like eggs, bread, and milk.
    *   This is in exchange for potential easing of packaging and healthy food regulations.
    *   Retailers are resisting, citing high operating costs and current food inflation of 3.7%.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o))
*   **UK Fuel Prices Hit Iran War Highs:**
    *   UK petrol prices reached 158.52p per litre, the highest since the US-Israel war with Iran began.
    *   Disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz have caused volatile Brent crude oil prices (currently $111/barrel).
    *   RAC warns prices could further rise, potentially impacting household energy bills from July.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c20zgjzz0e4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c20zgjzz0e4o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdep64555wzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdep64555wzo))
*   **UK Unemployment Rate Rises Unexpectedly:**
    *   The UK unemployment rate rose to 5% in the three months to March, with job vacancies at a five-year low.
    *   Analysts link this to the initial economic impact of the Iran war and weakening demand.
    *   Slowing wage growth may influence future Bank of England interest rate decisions.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czx2plkvg45o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czx2plkvg45o))
*   **NS&I to Repay Lost Funds:**
    *   UK's National Savings and Investments (NS&I) will contact 34,000 bereaved individuals regarding £367m in lost funds.
    *   Errors prevented full payout of deceased relatives' savings; funds will be repaid with interest.
    *   The repayment process is expected to be completed by the first half of next year.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgzrz59yz2o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgzrz59yz2o))

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Ebola Outbreak Spreading Rapidly in DR Congo:**
    *   The Ebola outbreak in northeastern DR Congo has claimed 136 lives, with over 514 suspected cases.
    *   The WHO warns cases may be spreading faster than estimated, with potential for substantial under-detection.
    *   Conflict in the region complicates control efforts; there is no vaccine for this specific Bundibugyo strain.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqp11gn1l8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqp11gn1l8o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgzj0pqpyyo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgzj0pqpyyo))
*   **Hantavirus Cases from Cruise Ship Confirmed:**
    *   A Canadian passenger from the MV Hondius cruise ship has tested positive for hantavirus, bringing total infections to 11.
    *   Three deaths are linked to the ship, with two confirmed to have had the virus.
    *   The WHO emphasizes a low pandemic risk for the Andes strain, as human-to-human transmission is rare.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno))
*   **Robotics Advancing Textile Manufacturing:**
    *   Companies are developing robots to automate clothes production, aiming to overcome challenges with floppy textiles.
    *   One firm uses adhesive-based methods to produce women's underwear and plans t-shirts for mass production next year.
    *   This innovation could re-shore manufacturing to Western countries and significantly reduce the environmental impact of the industry.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0q2gkj97eko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0q2gkj97eko))
