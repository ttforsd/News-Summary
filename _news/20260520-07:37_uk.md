## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **UK Loosens Russian Oil Sanctions Amid Fuel Price Hikes**:
    *   The UK government eased sanctions on Russian oil refined in third countries.
    *   This waiver applies to diesel and jet fuel, enabling imports from India and Turkey.
    *   Some sanctions on the maritime transport of Russian LNG were also lifted.
    *   The move addresses rising fuel prices and concerns over the Strait of Hormuz blockade.
    *   Critics argue the policy sends a negative signal about weakening sanctions on Russia.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy42x3g7r89o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy42x3g7r89o))

*   **UK Government Proposes Food Price Caps for Supermarkets**:
    *   The UK Treasury is urging major supermarkets to implement voluntary price caps on staple groceries.
    *   In return, retailers could receive an easing of packaging or healthy food regulations.
    *   Industry groups have strongly criticized the proposal as "1970s style price controls."
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o))

*   **HS2 High-Speed Rail Project Faces Further Delays and Cost Increases**:
    *   The estimated cost of the HS2 project has risen to between £87.7bn and £102.7bn.
    *   Trains on the London-Birmingham route are now expected to start running between 2036 and 2039.
    *   The top speed of the trains will be reduced to 320km/h to mitigate costs.
    *   The government describes this as a "reset" for the long-delayed project.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c794xw7p2dqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c794xw7p2dqo))

*   **UK Unemployment Rate Experiences Unexpected Rise**:
    *   The UK unemployment rate increased to 5% in the three months leading to March.
    *   Job vacancies declined to a five-year low of 705,000.
    *   These figures are seen as the initial impact of the Iran war on the labor market.
    *   Slowing wage growth may influence the Bank of England's future interest rate decisions.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czx2plkvg45o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czx2plkvg45o))

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Ebola Outbreak Worsens in DR Congo**:
    *   The World Health Organization (WHO) warns that the Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo is spreading faster than estimated.
    *   136 deaths and over 514 suspected cases have been reported in DR Congo, with one death in neighboring Uganda.
    *   The conflict-ridden region and population movements complicate containment efforts.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqp11gn1l8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqp11gn1l8o))

*   **Hantavirus Cases from Cruise Ship Under International Surveillance**:
    *   A Canadian passenger from the MV Hondius cruise ship tested positive for hantavirus, bringing the total to 11 infections and 3 deaths.
    *   Affected passengers are being repatriated globally, undergoing 42-day isolation periods.
    *   The WHO maintains that the overall risk of a widespread global outbreak remains low.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo))

*   **Google to Launch New AI-Integrated Smart Glasses**:
    *   Google plans to release new smart glasses this autumn, marking its return to the market after the Google Glass flop.
    *   The new devices will feature Gemini AI, a camera, and speakers for hands-free functionality.
    *   The launch is expected to reignite privacy concerns similar to those faced by Meta's smart glasses.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgz1ynq1nqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgz1ynq1nqo))

*   **International Crackdown on Online Fraud Networks**:
    *   A specialized international fraud squad made 31 arrests across Europe and Africa.
    *   The operation targeted organized networks involved in romance fraud and financial crime.
    *   Collaborative efforts involved the City of London Police, Cyber Defence Alliance, National Crime Agency, and Nigerian Police Force.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgjpnw8dg88o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgjpnw8dg88o))

*   **UK Advisers Call for Maximum Workplace Temperature Rules**:
    *   The UK's Climate Change Committee recommended introducing maximum temperature rules for workplaces.
    *   The recommendation aims to protect workers as heatwaves intensify due to climate change.
    *   The committee also urged prioritizing air conditioning in schools and hospitals.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2p1j4y0kro](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2p1j4y0kro))
