## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **UK Fuel Duty Extension:** UK extends the 5p fuel duty cut until year-end, costing £455m.
    *   Decision driven by rising fuel prices due to Middle East conflict.
    *   Includes cuts to red diesel duty and an HGV vehicle excise duty holiday.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3021md6jq6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3021md6jq6o))
*   **UK Supermarket Price Pressure:** Government urged supermarkets for voluntary price freezes on essentials.
    *   Retailers largely rejected the proposal as "preposterous."
    *   Food inflation was 3% in April; rising fertilizer costs are a factor.
    *   New CMA powers planned to tackle price gouging during crises.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o))
*   **UK HS2 Project Overruns:** HS2 high-speed rail project faces costs up to £102.7bn and further delays.
    *   Trains will run slower (320km/h vs 360km/h); project delayed to 2036-2039.
    *   Cost increases blamed on underestimates, inefficient delivery, and scope issues.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c794xw7p2dqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c794xw7p2dqo))
*   **UK Inflation Update:** UK inflation fell to 2.8% in April, but is expected to rise to 4% by year-end.
    *   Lower energy bills were the main driver of the drop.
    *   Rising fuel prices due to Middle East conflict are pushing costs up.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g0e0p4p2go](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g0e0p4p2go))

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Ebola Outbreak & Vaccine Efforts:** WHO warns a Bundibugyo Ebola vaccine could take nine months.
    *   600 suspected cases, 139 suspected deaths; 51 confirmed in DR Congo, 2 in Uganda.
    *   Emergency declared, but deemed low global pandemic risk.
    *   Current strain is rare; no approved vaccine, though two candidates are developing.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2g197dp8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2g197dp8o))
*   **Hantavirus Cruise Outbreak:** Canadian among 11 confirmed hantavirus cases from MV Hondius cruise.
    *   3 deaths linked; international isolation for returning passengers.
    *   WHO maintains low pandemic risk due to virus spread nature.
    *   No specific hantavirus treatment or widely available vaccine.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno))
*   **Google Smart Glasses Return:** Google will release new smart glasses this autumn, integrating Gemini AI.
    *   Features a camera and speakers for audio interaction with AI.
    *   Aims for "hands-free and heads-up" user experience.
    *   Re-entry after 2013 Google Glass flop; privacy concerns persist.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgz1ynq1nqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgz1ynq1nqo))
*   **Adenomyosis Awareness:** Increased awareness for Adenomyosis, a painful womb condition affecting 1 in 10 women.
    *   Symptoms are similar to endometriosis but confined to the womb muscle.
    *   Research aims for blood-sample diagnosis, potentially leading to at-home testing.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx21vgz77rgo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx21vgz77rgo))
