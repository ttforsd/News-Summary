## Executive Summary

*   **Middle East Tensions:** Iran asserts greater military control over the Strait of Hormuz; US continues blockade of Iranian ports.
*   **UK Economic Measures:** Chancellor announces temporary VAT cut for family attractions and meals, and extends fuel duty freeze, aiming to ease cost-of-living pressures.
*   **UK Migration Drop:** Net migration to the UK fell to 171,000 in 2025, nearly half of the previous year, marking the lowest since 2012.
*   **Tech Regulation:** UK's Ofcom criticizes TikTok and YouTube over child safety; Australia fines X (formerly Twitter) for failing to comply with child protection laws.
*   **Global Health Alerts:** WHO warns of a potentially faster-spreading Ebola outbreak in DR Congo; hantavirus cases confirmed from a cruise ship.
*   **US Politics/Economics:** Donald Trump receives immunity on past tax affairs; Walmart warns US consumer spending is slowing due to higher petrol prices.

## Major Regional Events

*   **Middle East Security Escalation**
    *   Iran claims expanded military control over 22,000 sq km around the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   This claim extends into Omani and UAE territorial waters.
    *   The UAE dismisses Iran's assertions as "fragments of dreams."
    *   The US rejects Iran's control claims and continues its blockade of Iranian ports.
    *   Footage released by IRGC-linked media appears to show a strike on a tanker. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq5py64gvwzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq5py64gvwzo))

*   **UK Policy and Social Issues**
    *   **Single-Sex Spaces Guidance:** The UK equalities watchdog confirms single-sex spaces, like toilets and changing rooms, must be based on biological sex.
        *   Transgender individuals should be offered third or gender-neutral facilities.
        *   The guidance follows a Supreme Court ruling and has been approved by ministers. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0e2rj3zj02o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0e2rj3zj02o))
    *   **Net Migration Decrease:** UK net migration decreased significantly to 171,000 in 2025.
        *   This is almost half the figure from 2024 and the lowest since 2012 (excluding the Covid pandemic).
        *   The drop is attributed to fewer non-EU arrivals for work and a 12% fall in asylum claims. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyp1ekd584o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyp1ekd584o))
    *   **Palantir Contract Blocked in London:** London’s Mayor's office blocked a £50m Met Police contract with Palantir for criminal investigation software.
        *   Concerns cited include value for money and the firm's ethics, though legal reasons for refusal focused on procurement process.
        *   The Met Police expressed disappointment, citing the need for modern technology. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyp1e11px0o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyp1e11px0o))
    *   **Prince Andrew's Trade Envoy Role:** Newly released government files confirm Queen Elizabeth II's strong desire for Prince Andrew to be a trade envoy in 2000.
        *   Documents reveal his preference for "sophisticated countries" and highlight careful media management. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pjg397l2o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pjg397l2o))
    *   **Labour Leadership Tax Proposal:** Former Health Secretary Wes Streeting proposes equalizing capital gains tax with income tax.
        *   Aims to create a "wealth tax that works" and could raise £12bn annually.
        *   This is part of his potential bid for the Labour leadership. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pxz7de41o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pxz7de41o))
    *   **Caribbean Land Disputes:** Local campaigners in Barbuda and Jamaica are fighting foreign developers over access to beaches.
        *   Disputes involve collective land ownership laws and the impact of luxury tourism developments.
        *   Robert de Niro is among investors in a resort that locals claim restricts access to ancestral coastlines. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62jp09p0l4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62jp09p0l4o))
    *   **Trump Tax Immunity & Cuban Indictment:** Former US President Donald Trump received immunity from future tax claims by the US Justice Department in a new settlement.
        *   Russia and China condemned the US indictment of former Cuban President Raúl Castro for a 1996 plane downing.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **UK Cost of Living Relief:** The UK Chancellor announced a temporary VAT cut from 20% to 5% for family attractions and children's meals this summer.
    *   This is part of a "Great British Summer Savings" campaign to ease household budgets.
    *   The government also extended the 5p fuel duty cut until the end of the year, costing £455m. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd7pzr88de1o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd7pzr88de1o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3021md6jq6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3021md6jq6o))
*   **Supermarket Price Pressure:** UK government attempts to pressure supermarkets into voluntarily freezing essential food prices were met with strong industry criticism.
    *   Supermarkets argue against state intervention, citing market competition.
    *   The Treasury is granting consumer protection watchdogs more power to tackle price gouging. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y7qz806q3o))
*   **EasyJet Fuel Supply & Bookings:** EasyJet CEO confirms summer flights will not be affected by jet fuel shortages despite Middle East conflict.

*   **UK-Gulf Trade Deal:** The UK secured a £3.7bn trade deal with six Gulf states (GCC).
    *   Aims to remove £580m in tariffs from British exports annually.
    *   Criticized by rights groups for lack of human rights and labor protections. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g85dryv24o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g85dryv24o))
*   **Russian Oil Sanctions:** The UK has reportedly "watered down" sanctions on Russian oil from third countries.
    *   Decision is attributed to concerns over rising fuel prices from the Iran war and supply issues. (Source: [https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/play/p0nm3wyl](https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/play/p0nm3wyl))
*   **Nvidia Stock Performance:** Nvidia reported record Q1 revenue of $81.6bn, driven by AI demand, but shares dropped 1.6%.
    *   Investors had high expectations, and competition concerns may have influenced the dip. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c759x9pq3dpo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c759x9pq3dpo))
*   **SpaceX IPO Plans:** Elon Musk's SpaceX is planning its largest-ever Wall Street IPO, potentially making him a trillionaire.

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Ebola Outbreak Warning:** The WHO warns that the Ebola outbreak in DR Congo may be spreading faster than estimated.
    *   136 deaths and over 514 suspected cases have been reported in the conflict-ridden Ituri province.
    *   Concerns rise about potential cross-border and inter-provincial dissemination. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqp11gn1l8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqp11gn1l8o))
*   **Hantavirus Cases Confirmed:** A Canadian passenger from the MV Hondius cruise ship tested positive for hantavirus.
    *   This brings the total confirmed cases to 11, with three associated deaths.
    *   Evacuated passengers are undergoing isolation and monitoring across several countries. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3r2p70jdy7o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3r2p70jdy7o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo))
*   **Hurricane Season Forecast:** NOAA predicts a below-average Atlantic hurricane season for 2026 (8-14 named storms).
    *   This is primarily due to a strengthening El Niño effect, expected to create strong wind shear.
    *   Despite the forecast, officials warn that even quiet seasons can produce devastating storms. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/weather/articles/cg5pg9prp3go](https://www.bbc.com/weather/articles/cg5pg9prp3go))
*   **STI Records in Europe:** Gonorrhoea and syphilis cases in Europe reached record highs in 2025.
    *   The ECDC attributes the surge partly to "widening gaps in testing and prevention."
    *   Calls for urgent public health action, including consistent condom use and increased testing. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pj07dr7lo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pj07dr7lo))
*   **Obesity Treatment Innovation:** A new daily pill, orforglipron, shows promise in helping patients maintain weight loss after discontinuing GLP-1 injections.
    *   Could offer a more convenient alternative to current injectable weight-loss drugs.
    *   Further research is needed on long-term treatment duration. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2e9wlvjxo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2e9wlvjxo))
*   **Online Child Safety Concerns:** UK regulator Ofcom states TikTok and YouTube are "not safe enough" for children.
    *   Ofcom is demanding stronger age verification and reduced exposure to harmful content.

(Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn0pky4zpxxo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn0pky4zpxxo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c626njg32n5o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c626njg32n5o))

    *   One sandwich was found to have more salt than the recommended daily limit for adults.
    *   Campaigners advocate for stricter salt reduction targets to combat public health risks like high blood pressure. (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g4l54g2ppo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g4l54g2ppo))
*   **Alcohol-Related Deaths:** UK alcohol-specific deaths fell in 2024 for the first time since the Covid pandemic.

    *   Proposals also include removing VAT on high-SPF sun creams to make them more affordable.
