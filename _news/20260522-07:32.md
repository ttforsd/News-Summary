## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Tensions Rise Amid Nuclear Standoff and Strait of Hormuz Claims**
    *   Iran has claimed expanded military control over 22,000 sq km of the Strait of Hormuz, including waters claimed by Oman and the UAE.
    *   The UAE denounced Iran's claims as "fragments of dreams," while the US and its allies reject any assertion of Iranian control over the strategic waterway.
    *   Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei reportedly directed that near-weapons-grade uranium not be sent abroad, complicating ongoing peace negotiations with the US.
    *   US Central Command boarded an Iran-bound oil tanker and has redirected 94 commercial ships since April 13, enforcing a blockade on Iranian ports.
    *   President Trump had previously postponed a planned military attack on Iran at the request of Gulf states to allow for negotiations.
    *   Pakistan is actively mediating peace talks between Washington and Tehran.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq5py64gvwzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cq5py64gvwzo), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/oil-prices-today-trump-iran-strait-of-hormuz-uranium-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/oil-prices-today-trump-iran-strait-of-hormuz-uranium-.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/iran-war-us-peace-talks-trump-hormuz.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/iran-war-us-peace-talks-trump-hormuz.html)

*   **US Pressure on Cuba Intensifies with Murder Charges Against Raúl Castro**
    *   US Secretary of State Marco Rubio labeled Cuba a "national security threat" and a "leading sponsor of terrorism."
    *   Cuba's Foreign Minister Bruno Rodríguez denied the accusations, calling them "lies" and an attempt to instigate military aggression.
    *   The US Department of Justice charged former Cuban President Raúl Castro with murder over the 1996 downing of two civilian planes.
    *   Cuba is experiencing severe fuel and food shortages, exacerbated by a US oil blockade, but accepted a US offer of $100 million in humanitarian aid.
    *   President Trump's administration is openly considering regime change in Cuba.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgpzwkn5jko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgpzwkn5jko), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgplwg3xk3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ckgplwg3xk3o), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/the-cuba-situation-is-starting-to-resemble-a-pre-conflict-playbook.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/the-cuba-situation-is-starting-to-resemble-a-pre-conflict-playbook.html)

## Major regional Events

*   **Israel Deportation of Gaza Flotilla Activists Sparks International Outcry**
    *   Israel deported hundreds of pro-Palestinian activists from the "Global Sumud Flotilla" after intercepting their vessels in international waters.
    *   A video showing an Israeli minister taunting handcuffed detainees drew international condemnation, including from the US, France, Italy, the UK, and Poland.

*   **DR Congo Grapples with Ebola Outbreak Amid Community Resistance and US Deportations**
    *   An angry crowd in eastern DR Congo set fire to Ebola isolation tents at Rwampara General Hospital after being prevented from conducting a traditional burial for a suspected victim.
    *   Community skepticism about the virus, viewing it as an "invention by outsiders," continues to hinder containment efforts, with many believing it is not real.
    *   The rare Bundibugyo Ebola species has caused 139 deaths among 600 suspected cases in DR Congo, with two cases detected in neighboring Uganda leading to border closures.
    *   The M23 rebel group has reported the first Ebola case in South Kivu, a region under their control, raising concerns about crisis management.
    *   Separately, the US deported 15 Latin American migrants and asylum seekers to DR Congo under a new agreement, with deportees reporting inhumane conditions and illness.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp8p2g8yp8do](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp8p2g8yp8do), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c78qw88jjd4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c78qw88jjd4o)

*   **Greenlanders Protest New US Consulate and Heightened US Influence**
    *   Hundreds of Greenlanders protested the inauguration of a new US consulate in Nuuk, asserting "Greenland is for Greenlanders" in response to increased US interest.
    *   The protests follow President Trump's previous efforts to purchase the autonomous Danish territory for strategic national security reasons.
    *   The US is seeking a greater military presence and the ability to veto major foreign investments in Greenland to counter Russian and Chinese influence.

*   **Alberta to Hold Referendum on Remaining in Canada**
    *   Alberta will hold a referendum on October 19 on whether the province should initiate a legal process to separate from Canada.
    *   The vote is fueled by a long-standing sense of being overlooked by the federal government and disputes over national policies impacting Alberta's oil and gas industry.
    *   Premier Danielle Smith, while personally advocating for Alberta to remain in Canada, supports the province's right to hold the vote.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgze8n5dxko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgze8n5dxko)

*   **Air France and Airbus Found Guilty of Manslaughter in 2009 AF447 Crash**
    *   A Paris Appeals Court found Air France and Airbus "solely and entirely responsible" for the 2009 Atlantic Ocean crash of Flight AF447, which killed all 228 people on board.
    *   Both companies were ordered to pay a maximum fine of €225,000 ($261,720; £194,500) and plan to appeal the verdict.
    *   The crash was attributed to a combination of technical failure in the aircraft's sensors and pilot error in responding to an aerodynamic stall.
    *   Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czd2qmdvmq6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/czd2qmdvmq6o)
