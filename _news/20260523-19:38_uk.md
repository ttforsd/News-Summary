## Major Regional Events

*   **US-Iran Relations Show Progress**
    *   US President Trump indicated Iran is "getting closer" to an agreement.
    *   Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman confirmed converging positions last week.
    *   Initial proposals would exclude nuclear weapons discussions.
    *   Trump insisted any deal must prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon.
    *   US Secretary of State Rubio expects updates soon, emphasizing nuclear weapon prevention and Strait of Hormuz reopening.
    *   Mood had previously soured, with talks of military strikes; a temporary ceasefire began in April.
    *   US has blockaded Iranian ports since April 13, redirecting 100 vessels.
    *   Iran claims military control of Strait of Hormuz, demanding authorization for transit.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crmp121z3z8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crmp121z3z8o)

*   **US Considering Cuba Policy Options**
    *   The US administration is evaluating options for Cuba, including potential military action.
    *   An oil blockade has severely impacted Cuba's economy, raising humanitarian concerns.
    *   Former President Raul Castro was indicted for murder, and Cuban military leaders sanctioned.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/play/w3ct8lz3](https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/play/w3ct8lz3)

*   **French Border Checks Suspended at Dover**
    *   French authorities briefly suspended extra EU border checks at Dover due to extensive queues.
    *   Thousands of holidaymakers faced hours of delays during the UK's May bank holiday.
    *   Delays were caused by new EU digital Entry/Exit System (EES) preparations.
    *   French authorities reinstated additional checks later on Saturday.
    *   The EES will eventually require biometric checks for non-EU travelers in 29 Schengen countries.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp3p2ver9k6o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp3p2ver9k6o)

*   **Deadly Coal Mine Explosion in China**
    *   An explosion at Liushenyu Coal Mine in Shanxi province killed at least 82 people.
    *   Two workers remain missing; 128 were hospitalized, two in critical condition.
    *   This is China's worst mining disaster since 2009.
    *   Chinese President Xi Jinping called for no effort to be spared in rescue and treatment.
    *   A "rigorous" investigation is underway, with management team members detained.
    *   The mine was previously listed for "severe safety hazards" in 2024.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y0ve18qlko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y0ve18qlko)

*   **UK Youth Unemployment Disparity Highlighted**
    *   Former minister Alan Milburn stated UK spends 25 times more on youth benefits than job support.
    *   Nearly a million young people (16-24) are not in education, employment, or training (NEET).
    *   Milburn's report calls for a "system reset" of welfare, school, skills, and health systems.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crrpx4p1z71o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/crrpx4p1z71o)

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **US Federal Reserve Independence Emphasized**
    *   President Trump urged incoming Fed chair Kevin Warsh to be "totally independent".
    *   Trump asked Warsh to ignore outside opinions and "let the economy boom."
    *   This follows Trump's public clashes with previous Fed chair Jerome Powell over interest rates.
    *   The Fed held rates in April; economists anticipate no cuts in 2026.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgz311dq3ko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgz311dq3ko)

*   **UK Public Sector Borrowing at Post-Covid High**
    *   UK public sector borrowing in April reached £24.3bn, the highest since the Covid-19 pandemic.
    *   This figure is £4.9bn higher than last year and exceeded expectations.
    *   Higher benefit spending and state pension increases contributed significantly.
    *   Debt interest payments for April hit a record £10.3bn.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce9py7nx8j4o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce9py7nx8j4o)

*   **SpaceX Postpones Starship Launch Amid IPO Plans**
    *   Elon Musk's SpaceX postponed its Starship rocket test flight due to a hydraulic pin malfunction.
    *   The delay occurred a day after plans for a record-breaking Nasdaq IPO were revealed.
    *   Successful launch could boost investor interest in the stock market debut (SPCX).
    *   Musk's stake in SpaceX could make him the world's first trillionaire.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy42w782ejvo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy42w782ejvo)

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **DR Congo Ebola Outbreak Worsens, Vaccine Efforts Underway**
    *   WHO raised the public health risk in DR Congo to "very high" due to the Bundibugyo Ebola strain.
    *   Three Red Cross volunteers died from suspected Ebola, likely contracted from body management.
    *   Scientists at Oxford University are developing a new Bundibugyo vaccine, aiming for clinical trials in months.
    *   The outbreak has caused 177 suspected deaths and 750 suspected cases; Uganda also reported cases.
    *   Violence and insecurity in the conflict-ridden region hinder response efforts.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c759knxln0wo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c759knxln0wo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr7p30m1dn1o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr7p30m1dn1o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy82gkr7xzlo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy82gkr7xzlo)

*   **Record STI Levels in Europe**
    *   Gonorrhoea and syphilis cases in Europe reached their highest levels in over 10 years in 2024.
    *   Gonorrhoea cases increased by 303% since 2015 to 106,331.
    *   Syphilis cases more than doubled to 45,557 in the same period.
    *   ECDC attributes surge to "widening gaps in testing and prevention."
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pj07dr7lo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cm2pj07dr7lo)

*   **Hantavirus Case Confirmed in Canada**
    *   A Canadian from the MV Hondius cruise ship tested positive for hantavirus, developing mild symptoms.
    *   The case brings total infections to 11, with 3 deaths linked to the outbreak.
    *   Authorities believe the Andes strain of hantavirus, carried by rodents, was contracted in South America.
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2k2p057ezno), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g8318v4yzo)

*   **AI Accelerates Drug Discovery for Brain Conditions**
    *   Scientists at the UK Dementia Research Institute use AI to identify treatments for neurological conditions.
    *   Algorithms analyze patient data and lab-grown brain cells to repurpose existing drugs.
    *   This approach aims to find effective treatments in "years rather than decades."
    *   Sources: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdrp3zzzp71o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdrp3zzzp71o)
