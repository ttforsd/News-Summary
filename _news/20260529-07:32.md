## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Ceasefire Negotiations Face Hurdles Amid Escalations**
    *   US Vice-President JD Vance states a 60-day ceasefire extension framework is "very close" but "not there yet."
    *   Sticking points include Iran's nuclear program, particularly uranium enrichment and disposal of stockpiles.
    *   Proposed deal includes lifting US naval blockade and sanctions, allowing Iran to sell oil and for unrestricted passage through the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Iran’s semi-official Tasnim news agency disputed deal finalization; the White House called Iran's reported memorandum draft a "complete fabrication."
    *   **Recent Hostilities:** Iran reportedly fired missiles at unidentified targets and attacked a US air base in the region.
    *   **US Response:** US Central Command reported intercepting an Iranian ballistic missile over Kuwait and five attack drones near the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   **US Sanctions:** The US Treasury sanctioned Iran's "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" for attempting to control Strait transit.
    *   **Warning to Oman:** Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent warned Oman against facilitating tolls in the Strait of Hormuz, saying partners would be penalized.
    *   **Outlook:** President Trump remains "not satisfied" with terms, warns that "option B" (return to combat) is still on the table.

## Major regional Events

*   **Israel Escalates Control in Gaza and Strikes Lebanon**
    *   **Gaza Strip:** Israeli PM Netanyahu directed the IDF to increase control of Gaza to 70%, up from the current 60%, directly contradicting the October 2025 ceasefire terms.
    *   **Gaza Casualties:** An Israeli strike in Gaza City killed at least 10 people, including five children, targeting a Hamas battalion commander.
    *   **Hamas Leaders Targeted:** Recent Israeli strikes killed Hamas military wing head Mohammed Odeh and other key figures involved in weapons manufacturing and fund transfers.
    *   **Lebanon Strikes:** Israel conducted a "targeted strike" in Beirut, its second since the ceasefire, reportedly targeting the head of an Iranian militia allied with Hezbollah.
    *   **Evacuation Orders:** The IDF issued large-scale evacuation orders for residents in southern Lebanon up to the Zahrani River, amidst intensifying strikes.
    *   **Ceasefire Violations:** Both Israel and Hezbollah accuse each other of violating the fragile ceasefire, raising fears of a broader conflict.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqpelq5reqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ceqpelq5reqo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn9p40en4ngo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn9p40en4ngo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgqpjwdv7xeo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgqpjwdv7xeo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0r2ydlvk41o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c0r2ydlvk41o))

*   **Russian Drone Hits NATO Member Romania**
    *   A Russian drone crashed into an apartment building in Galati, Romania, causing a fire and injuring two people.
    *   This marks the first time Romanian citizens have been hurt by a Russian drone since the Ukraine war began in February 2022.
    *   Romania's foreign ministry condemned the incident as a "serious and irresponsible escalation" and informed NATO, requesting accelerated transfer of anti-drone capabilities.
    *   NATO condemned Russia's "recklessness" and pledged to strengthen defenses.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c93x4nxlkjeo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c93x4nxlkjeo))

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Global Markets Rally on Tech, Shrug off Iran Tensions**
    *   S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite closed at new record highs, boosted by a broad rally in the technology sector.
    *   South Korea's Kospi and Japan's Topix also hit record highs.
    *   Investors largely overlooked renewed US-Iran military actions, focusing instead on strong tech earnings.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/cnbc-daily-open-tech-outshines-geopolitical-tensions-on-wall-street.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/cnbc-daily-open-tech-outshines-geopolitical-tensions-on-wall-street.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/south-koreas-kospi-japans-topix-hit-record-highs-as-investors-shrug-off-iran-tensions.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/south-koreas-kospi-japans-topix-hit-record-highs-as-investors-shrug-off-iran-tensions.html))

*   **US Inflation Persists, Fed Eyes Rate Decisions**
    *   US core Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) price index rose 0.2% monthly and 3.3% annually in April, meeting expectations.
    *   Headline inflation reached 3.8% annually, the highest since May 2023.
    *   First-quarter GDP growth was revised down to 1.6% annualized.
    *   Chicago Fed President Austan Goolsbee highlighted that energy inflation from the Iran war is more persistent than anticipated, potentially leading to a "stagflationary shock" in Asia.
    *   Minneapolis Fed President Neel Kashkari reiterated that curbing inflation is the Fed's top priority, as the labor market is "in decent shape."
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/core-inflation-hit-an-annual-rate-of-3point3percent-in-april-as-expected-feds-preferred-gauge-shows-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/core-inflation-hit-an-annual-rate-of-3point3percent-in-april-as-expected-feds-preferred-gauge-shows-.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/energy-inflation-has-been-more-persistent-than-expected-feds-goolsbee-tells-cnbc.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/energy-inflation-has-been-more-persistent-than-expected-feds-goolsbee-tells-cnbc.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/fed-reserve-neel-kashkari-inflation-job-market.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/fed-reserve-neel-kashkari-inflation-job-market.html))

*   **ECB Faces Rate Hike Dilemma Amidst Fragile Economy**
    *   The European Central Bank (ECB) is poised to hike interest rates by 25 basis points in June, bringing the deposit facility rate to 2.25%.
    *   Market expectations of these hikes are already tightening financial and lending conditions in the Eurozone.
    *   Goldman Sachs indicates that a quarter of the economic drag is exogenous to monetary policy expectations, suggesting caution in further tightening.
    *   Euro area inflation stood at 3% in April, fueled partly by the Iran war.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/the-ecb-is-debating-rate-hikes-but-the-private-sector-could-help-out.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/the-ecb-is-debating-rate-hikes-but-the-private-sector-could-help-out.html))

*   **EU Fines Temu €200M for Illegal Products**
    *   The European Union has fined Chinese online retailer Temu €200 million for allowing the sale of illegal products on its platform.
    *   Investigation found dangerous baby toys and faulty chargers, indicating failure to assess systemic risks.
    *   Temu disputes the fine and is developing an action plan to address the deficiencies.
    *   This is the second fine under the EU's Digital Services Act, aiming to send a "very strong message" to online platforms.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1k2ydn1rz8o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1k2ydn1rz8o))

*   **India's Economy Buffeted by Iran War, Domestic Tourism Rises**
    *   The Iran war is causing economic strain for India, including a weakening currency and rising import bill.
    *   Prime Minister Modi encouraged citizens to curb overseas travel, inadvertently boosting domestic tourism.
    *   Indian hospitality sector reports increased bookings for local destinations, with some firms seeing nearly 40% rise in May.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/iran-war-disruptions-modis-appeal-to-boost-indias-hospitality-sector.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/iran-war-disruptions-modis-appeal-to-boost-indias-hospitality-sector.html))
