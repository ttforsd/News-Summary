## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **DNA Test Reduces Chemotherapy for Breast Cancer**
    *   A new DNA test, Prosigna, can identify breast cancer patients who can safely forgo chemotherapy.
    *   An international study found that over two-thirds of participants avoided harsh side effects.
    *   The test could spare over 5,000 NHS patients annually from unnecessary treatment.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2325j0xk1vo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c2325j0xk1vo))

*   **Limited Prostate Cancer Screening Recommended**
    *   UK scientific advisors recommend prostate cancer screening only for a "few thousand" high-risk men.
    *   Targeted individuals are those with a BRCA2 genetic variant and a family history of cancer, aged 45-61.
    *   Broader screening is not advised, as potential harms currently outweigh the benefits for most groups.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn0pvxe5jgzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cn0pvxe5jgzo))

*   **UK Scientists Develop New Ebola Vaccine**
    *   Oxford University scientists are accelerating development of a vaccine for the rare Bundibugyo Ebola species.
    *   The vaccine, utilizing ChAdOx1 technology, could be ready for clinical trials within months.
    *   This development comes as the WHO upgrades the Ebola risk in DR Congo to "very high."
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy82gkr7xzlo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy82gkr7xzlo), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr7p30m1dn1o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cr7p30m1dn1o))

*   **Measles Outbreak Causes Child Deaths in Bangladesh**
    *   Over 500 children have died in Bangladesh since March due to a rapidly spreading measles outbreak.
    *   UNICEF reports overwhelmed hospitals and significant delays in vaccine procurement contributing to the crisis.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2d5d7wn5o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy2d5d7wn5o))

*   **PCOS Renamed for Better Medical Understanding**
    *   Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) has been renamed to Polyendocrine Metabolic Ovarian Syndrome (PMOS).
    *   The change aims to more accurately reflect the condition's systemic metabolic and endocrine aspects, improving diagnosis and treatment.
