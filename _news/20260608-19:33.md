## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **Middle East Tensions & US Diplomacy**
    *   Iran and Israel ceased direct strikes following a weekend exchange, the first since the April ceasefire.
        *   Iran attributed its missile launch to Israeli truce violations in Lebanon; Israel retaliated against military targets in Iran.
        *   Iranian military warned of harsher responses if Israeli "aggression and malice" continues, especially in Lebanon.
        *   Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu affirmed the conflict with Iran and Hezbollah "has not yet ended."
    *   US President Donald Trump urged an "immediate CEASEFIRE!" and called for swift progress in peace negotiations.
        *   The 100-day conflict has contributed to global oil price volatility and increased airline fuel costs.

## Major Regional Events

*   **Philippines Earthquake Devastation**
    *   A powerful magnitude-7.8 earthquake struck off the coast of Mindanao, southern Philippines.
        *   At least 35 fatalities, 134 injuries, and 10,000 families displaced have been reported.
        *   Tsunami alerts were issued for regional countries, though later downgraded or cancelled.
        *   The quake caused significant building collapses and landslides in affected areas.
        *   *(Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyel78e6p5o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/clyel78e6p5o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/videos/cvgdxqwlrpyo](https://www.bbc.com/news/videos/cvgdxqwlrpyo))*
*   **Armenian Election Solidifies Pro-West Stance**
    *   Armenian Prime Minister Nikol Pashinyan's Civil Contract Party secured a victory in a critical election.
        *   The win reinforces Armenia's strategic shift towards closer ties with the West.
        *   Russia's Foreign Ministry criticized the election, alleging Western interference and pressure on opposition.
        *   *(Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgel990n51o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgel990n51o))*
*   **Rising Xenophobia in South Africa**
    *   Malawi repatriated 150 citizens from South Africa amidst escalating xenophobia concerns.
        *   This follows violence in Western Cape Province, with other African nations also arranging repatriations.
        *   Anti-migrant groups have issued a June 30 deadline for undocumented migrants to leave the country.
        *   South African President Ramaphosa announced measures to combat illegal migration while condemning xenophobia.
        *   *(Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cnv9dz3reego](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cnv9dz3reego))*
*   **Boko Haram Hostage Release in Nigeria**
    *   Over 360 individuals, abducted by Boko Haram in March, were freed from a mountain hideout in Borno state.
        *   Accounts vary between an army-led operation and local mediation efforts for their release.
        *   Tragically, two infants died from exhaustion during their prolonged captivity.
        *   *(Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8j0jn4xjgo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8j0jn4xjgo))*
*   **Peruvian Presidential Election Uncertainty**
    *   Peru's presidential election remains in a statistical tie, with final recounts expected to take weeks.
        *   Left-wing Roberto Sánchez is narrowly leading right-wing Keiko Fujimori.
        *   Insecurity and crime emerged as dominant voter concerns throughout the campaign.
        *   Deep political polarization is anticipated, with potential protests from Sánchez supporters if he does not win.
        *   *(Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cewq151pw78o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cewq151pw78o), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9d21184e7yo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c9d21184e7yo))*

    *   Former Chief Justice David Maraga was arrested in Nairobi while protesting alleged construction on national park land.
        *   Activists oppose plans for a car park and an expanded animal orphanage within the Nairobi National Park.
        *   Amnesty International condemned the arrests, citing "violent dispersal" of peaceful demonstrators.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

    *   Asian tech markets experienced a sharp sell-off, with South Korea's Kospi plunging over 8% at opening, triggering a trading halt.
        *   This downturn followed a disappointing AI chip sales forecast from Broadcom and concerns about AI investment overvaluation.
        *   Major chipmakers and tech firms in Japan and South Korea, including Samsung and Softbank, saw significant share drops.
        *   US Nasdaq and S&P 500 recovered some losses, but market jitters persist.

*   **Oil Market & Inflation Concerns**
    *   Global oil prices initially surged then stabilized after the Iran-Israel exchanges, but remain significantly higher than pre-war levels.
        *   Brent crude is 36% and West Texas Intermediate (WTI) is 50% above their respective pre-conflict prices.
        *   China's reduced crude imports have played a key role in moderating global price increases.
        *   Analysts predict eventual price hikes as global oil inventories deplete and strategic reserves are rebuilt.
    *   Airline profits are forecast to halve in 2026 due to a $100 billion surge in jet fuel costs.
        *   Rising energy expenses are contributing to increased inflation across major economies.
        *   A shortage of essential components like resin could lead to higher prices for electronic devices this autumn.
