## Executive summary

*   The US and Iran have reached a preliminary agreement to end their four-month conflict, aiming to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.
*   Global markets responded positively, with oil prices falling and Asian tech stocks rallying due to reduced geopolitical tensions.
*   Ukraine reported 11 killed in Russian strikes, which also damaged Kyiv's historic Dormition Cathedral.
*   SpaceX completed a record-setting $85.7 billion IPO, achieving a valuation exceeding $2 trillion.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Peace Agreement Advances**
    *   The US and Iran have reached a preliminary deal to end their four-month conflict.
    *   The agreement includes an immediate end to military operations across all fronts, including Lebanon.
    *   It is expected to be formally signed in Geneva on Friday.
    *   The Strait of Hormuz is slated to reopen, and the US naval blockade will be lifted.
    *   Future negotiations are planned to address Iran's nuclear program and sanctions relief.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdr4x3vg347o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdr4x3vg347o), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/us-iran-deal-hormuz-markets.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/us-iran-deal-hormuz-markets.html), [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/vance-says-us-expects-strait-of-hormuz-to-be-open-toll-free-long-term.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/vance-says-us-expects-strait-of-hormuz-to-be-open-toll-free-long-term.html), [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd952n4qz2qo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cd952n4qz2qo))
*   **International Support for Iran Deal**
    *   World leaders have welcomed the US-Iran agreement, urging quick implementation and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   European nations (UK, France, Germany, Italy) have indicated readiness to lift relevant sanctions in exchange for verifiable nuclear program curbs.
    *   The UN Secretary-General congratulated the parties, highlighting its importance for regional peace.
    *   (Source: [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/world-leaders-welcome-us-iran-deal-as-europe-signals-sanctions-relief-urges-hormuz-reopening.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/06/15/world-leaders-welcome-us-iran-deal-as-europe-signals-sanctions-relief-urges-hormuz-reopening.html))
*   **Israeli Disagreement with Iran Deal**
    *   Israel, not a party to the US-Iran agreement, expressed strong opposition and dismay.
    *   Israeli Defense Minister stated forces would maintain presence in security zones in Lebanon, Gaza, and Syria.
    *   Prime Minister Netanyahu faces internal political pressure regarding US relations and his security policies.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4gnqw8j52o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4gnqw8j52o))

## Major regional Events

*   **Renewed Russian Strikes in Ukraine**
    *   Russian strikes killed at least 11 people, including five rescue workers in Kharkiv.
    *   Kyiv’s 11th-Century Dormition Cathedral, a Unesco World Heritage site, was significantly damaged.
    *   Ukraine reported 70 missiles and 611 drones were launched in the overnight attack.
    *   A Ukrainian drone attack in the Russian city of Tula reportedly killed three people.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gy037gjz3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4gy037gjz3o))
*   **China Detains Underground Church Leaders**
    *   Two leaders of the Early Rain Covenant Church were detained in Jiangyou, China.
    *   Over 30 congregants were interrogated by armed police during a Sunday service.
    *   This action reflects the Chinese Communist Party's stringent control over religious groups.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce9582vlm83o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce9582vlm83o))
*   **Swiss Voters Reject Population Cap**
    *   Swiss voters rejected a proposal to cap the country's population at 10 million, with 55% voting against it.
    *   The initiative was put forth by the right-wing Swiss People's Party (SVP).
    *   The government and business leaders opposed the cap, citing potential harm to EU free movement agreements and exacerbation of labor shortages.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c20ygjem17zo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c20ygjem17zo))
*   **MSF Staff Implicated in Refugee Abuse Scandal**
    *   Medical charity Doctors Without Borders (MSF) reported that its staff sexually abused at least 59 Sudanese refugees in Chad in 2024.
    *   Offences involved exchanging food or jobs for sex, with some cases possibly constituting "sexual trafficking."
    *   MSF has terminated 18 staff members, acknowledging victims' fear of reporting due to concerns about aid access.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74ykjl8mx3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74ykjl8mx3o))
*   **Refugee Students Scammed in Finland Scheme**
    *   Burmese refugees seeking education abroad were defrauded by the "Brighter Future Way" agency.
    *   Students paid substantial fees for vocational school enrollment in Finland but were subsequently denied residence permits.
    *   Finnish authorities are investigating the agency for "aggravated extortion."
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgrprx55zpvo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgrprx55zpvo))
*   **Delhi Endures Severe Heatwave**
    *   India's capital, Delhi, is grappling with a severe heatwave, with air temperatures consistently above 40C.
    *   Thermal cameras recorded surface temperatures reaching as high as 64C in some urban areas.
    *   This highlights significant public health risks, particularly for outdoor workers.
    *   (Source: [https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62173jqd4wo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c62173jqd4wo))
