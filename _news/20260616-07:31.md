## Executive summary

*   **US-Iran Preliminary Deal:** A Memorandum of Understanding has been signed, aiming to end the four-month war, reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and establish a framework for nuclear negotiations.
*   **Japan's Interest Rate Hike:** The Bank of Japan raised its policy rate to 1%, the highest since 1995, responding to inflation and yen weakness.
*   **China's Economic Slowdown:** Retail sales dropped for the first time in over three years in May, alongside a contraction in urban fixed-asset investment.
*   **Ukraine War Intensifies:** Russian strikes killed 11 people and damaged a historic cathedral in Kyiv, while Ukraine reported drone attacks on Russian territory.
*   **AI Policy & Investment:** Qualcomm projects AI agents will replace traditional apps, while Anthropic faces a US government directive restricting access to its AI models due to national security concerns.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Peace Framework Signed:** A preliminary Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to end the four-month US-Iran war has been signed by US President Trump, Vice-President Vance, and Iran's Parliamentary Speaker Ghalibaf.
    *   Details: The MOU is "about a page and a half" and "very general," establishing a 60-day ceasefire extension for detailed negotiations.
    *   Strait of Hormuz: The agreement includes the reopening of the strategic Strait of Hormuz, with US officials expecting toll-free passage long-term.
    *   Nuclear Program: Iran has committed to a verifiable plan to not build nuclear weapons and destroy enriched material, with technical talks expected this week.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8mv6l6eezo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8mv6l6eezo)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/us-iran-deal-hormuz-markets.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/us-iran-deal-hormuz-markets.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/vance-iran-deal-negotiations-nuclear.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/vance-iran-deal-negotiations-nuclear.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/vp-vance-says-us-expects-strait-of-hormuz-to-be-open-toll-free-long-term.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/vp-vance-says-us-expects-strait-of-hormuz-to-be-open-toll-free-long-term.html))
*   **International Reaction to Iran Deal:** World leaders, including China and European nations (UK, France, Germany, Italy), welcomed the US-Iran agreement.
    *   Support: Many urged rapid implementation and unconditional reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Sanctions: Some European countries signaled readiness to lift sanctions pending verifiable steps on Iran's nuclear program.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/world-leaders-welcome-us-iran-deal-as-europe-signals-sanctions-relief-urges-hormuz-reopening.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/06/15/europe-japan-countries-welcome-us-iran-deal-strait-of-hormuz-reopening.html))
*   **Israel's Stance on Deal:** Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu stated forces would remain in security zones in Lebanon, Syria, and Gaza.
    *   Action: He reiterated Israel's right to self-defense and commitment to preventing Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons.
    *   Impact: The deal presents a "political nightmare" for Netanyahu, isolating him from his US ally.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8mv6l6eezo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8mv6l6eezo)), ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4gnqw8j52o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cj4gnqw8j52o)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/us-iran-deal-hormuz-markets.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/us-iran-deal-hormuz-markets.html))
*   **G7 Summit in France:** US President Trump is attending the G7 summit in Évian-les-Bains, France, where the Iran deal is a key discussion point.
    *   Tariff Threats: Trump warned France of 100% wine tariffs if it does not scrap its digital services tax on US tech companies.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8mv6l6eezo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce8mv6l6eezo)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/cnbc-daily-open-iran-framework-signed-but-not-delivered.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/cnbc-daily-open-iran-framework-signed-but-not-delivered.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/trump-france-tech-tax-wine-tariffs-champagne.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/trump-france-tech-tax-wine-tariffs-champagne.html))

## Major Regional Events

    *   Infrastructure: Strikes left over 140,000 in Kyiv without electricity; Ukraine reports 70 missiles and 611 drones.

    *   Mission: The aircraft was on a routine test mission supporting a radar modernization program.
    *   Investigation: Cause not yet determined; initial footage suggests it was "unrecoverable and unsurvivable."

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Japan Raises Interest Rates:** The Bank of Japan hiked its policy rate to 1% from 0.75%, the highest level since 1995, amidst rising inflation and a weak yen.
    *   Context: This marks a shift from decades of near-zero rates to combat deflation.
    *   Impact: Nikkei 225 climbed, yen strengthened slightly, bond yields rose.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cjdgl213dpzo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cjdgl213dpzo)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/bank-of-japan-hikes-rates-to-1-highest-since-1995-as-yen-and-inflation-worries-take-hold-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/bank-of-japan-hikes-rates-to-1-highest-since-1995-as-yen-and-inflation-worries-take-hold-.html))
*   **China's Economic Indicators Weaken:** Retail sales in China declined 0.6% in May, the first drop in over three years, missing expectations.
    *   Investment: Urban fixed-asset investment contracted 4.1%, dragged by real estate and manufacturing.
    *   Bright Spot: Industrial output rose 4.5%, exceeding estimates.
    *   Outlook: Signals ongoing domestic imbalance of strong supply and weak demand, increasing pressure for further stimulus.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/china-economy-may-retail-sales-industrial-output-fixed-asset-investment-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/china-economy-may-retail-sales-industrial-output-fixed-asset-investment-.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/cnbcs-the-china-connection-newsletter-waiting-for-ai-to-lift-the-whole-market.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/cnbcs-the-china-connection-newsletter-waiting-for-ai-to-lift-the-whole-market.html))
*   **Oil Market Volatility:** Oil prices fell sharply due to the US-Iran deal but analysts warn of continued volatility and a "geopolitical risk premium."
    *   Outlook: Global oil inventories are depleted, Strait of Hormuz mines pose a risk, and ship maintenance could prolong recovery for months.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/oil-will-still-cause-volatility-even-with-us-iran-deal-analysts-.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/oil-will-still-cause-volatility-even-with-us-iran-deal-analysts-.html)), ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/how-the-strait-of-hormuz-reopening-could-unfold-if-the-u.s.-iran-deal-is-implemented.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/how-the-strait-of-hormuz-reopening-could-unfold-if-the-u.s.-iran-deal-is-implemented.html))

## Science, Tech, & Health (Major Developments)

*   **Qualcomm CEO on AI Agents:** Qualcomm CEO Cristiano Amon predicts AI agents will replace traditional apps and envisions over 40 new AI-powered devices.
    *   Innovation: These devices include wearable tech like jewelry, earbuds with cameras, pins, and watches.
    *   Shift: Amon believes AI agents will become the "new app," shifting the "center of gravity" from smartphones.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/qualcomm-ceo-ai-devices-agents.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/qualcomm-ceo-ai-devices-agents.html))
*   **Anthropic Faces US Directive:** AI company Anthropic is meeting with the Trump administration to resolve a directive suspending foreign national access to its AI models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5.
    *   Reason: The directive cites "national security authorities" due to a "potential narrow, non-universal jailbreak."
    *   Industry Impact: Anthropic argues such a standard would halt new model deployments across the industry.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/anthropic-mythos-trump-ai.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/anthropic-mythos-trump-ai.html))
*   **Biotech IPO Market Reopening:** Public markets for biotech companies are starting to reopen after muted activity, but Big Pharma M&A still drives the pace.
    *   Trend: Many biotechs pursue dual-track processes (IPO prep and acquisition talks) due to selective investors and Big Pharma's need to refill pipelines.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/biotech-deals-ipo-pharma-dealmaking-2026.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/biotech-deals-ipo-pharma-dealmaking-2026.html))
*   **MSF Staff Accused of Abuse:** Doctors Without Borders (MSF) admitted staff sexually abused at least 59 Sudanese refugees in eastern Chad.
    *   Details: Exploitation included sex-for-food or jobs, with some victims being young girls; 18 culprits were sacked.
    *   Context: Occurred in 2024, a year into Sudan's civil war, which is recognized as the world's worst humanitarian crisis.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74ykjl8mx3o](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c74ykjl8mx3o))
*   **Ebola Outbreak in DR Congo:** Health workers celebrate successful Ebola patient treatments in DR Congo's Ituri province, the epicentre of the latest outbreak.
    *   Challenge: Over 140 deaths from the Bundibugyo species; authorities battle local myths and misinformation hindering containment efforts.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp3xyvww1lqo](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cp3xyvww1lqo))
*   **Trust in News at All-Time Low:** Global public trust in news has fallen to 37%, the lowest since 2015, according to the Reuters Institute.
    *   Consumption Shift: Over half of respondents get news from social media/video, though traditional sources remain popular in the UK.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgq1py92eko](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cvgq1py92eko))
*   **UK Social Media Ban for Under-16s:** The UK will ban social media platforms from offering services to under-16s, aiming to protect children online.
    *   Policy: Modeled on Australian legislation but with stricter features like blocking livestreaming for minors.
    *   Challenges: Experts warn of enforcement difficulties due to workarounds like VPNs and potential confusion for tech companies.
    *   Sources: ([https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/uk-social-media-ban-under-16s.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/15/uk-social-media-ban-under-16s.html))
