## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Interim Peace Deal Signed, Talks Stall:** The US lifted its naval blockade of Iran following an interim Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to end the Middle East war, but follow-up talks scheduled in Switzerland were cancelled due to logistical issues.
    *   Iran's Supreme Leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, approved the deal but characterized Trump's action as "out of desperation."
    *   The MOU includes reopening the Strait of Hormuz, Iran's commitment not to pursue nuclear weapons, and a proposed $300 billion reconstruction fund for Iran (US not obligated to contribute).
    *   A 60-day (extendable) period for negotiating a final nuclear agreement and the disposition of enriched uranium stockpiles has begun.
    *   US Vice President JD Vance defended the deal, asserting no direct US funding to Iran and conditional economic benefits based on compliance.
    *   Critics, including Israeli cabinet members, expressed concerns over concessions and Iran's continued backing of regional proxy groups.

*   **NATO Faces US Scrutiny:** US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth sharply criticized some NATO allies as "free-riding" and announced a six-month "NATO 3.0" review of US forces in Europe.
    *   Hegseth condemned allies who refused US base access for Iran war strikes and for failing to meet defense spending targets (3.5% of GDP for core defense).
    *   The review aims to accelerate Europe's leadership in its own continental security.
    *   [Sources: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3vy5l62622o, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/hegseth-nato-defense-review-fail-europe.html]
*   **US-India Relations Improve:** US President Donald Trump announced a future visit to India, signaling a warming of relations after recent tensions over tariffs and a maritime incident.
    *   Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi raised concerns about the safety of Indian sailors with Trump at the G7 summit.
    *   [Source: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c621glv9vnqo]
*   **Japan Boosts Defense Capabilities:** Japan's Defence Minister Shinjiro Koizumi emphasized that strengthening defense and considering a revision of the pacifist Article 9 of the constitution are "critical" for regional stability.
    *   Japan is relaxing arms export rules, increasing defense spending to 2% of GDP, and investing in new missile and drone technologies.
    *   China and North Korea's military actions are key drivers behind this shift.

## Global Economic & Market Movers

*   **Strait of Hormuz Reopens Partially:** The Strait of Hormuz has begun reopening after the US-Iran deal, with several Saudi oil tankers already transiting.
    *   Oil prices initially dipped below $80 per barrel, but industry experts caution that a full normalization of shipping could take weeks or months.
    *   This delay is due to backlogs, mine clearance, and unresolved issues like potential Iranian service charges.
    *   OPEC dismissed the IEA's forecast of a 2027 oil supply glut, highlighting market volatility from unverified assumptions.
    *   [Sources: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/strait-hormuz-reopening-shipping-backlog-oil.html, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/three-saudi-oil-tankers-carrying-6-million-barrels-cross-strait-of-hormuz.html, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/opec-iea-supply-glut-forecast-critical-strait-hormuz-reopens-oil.html]
*   **Apple to Raise Product Prices Due to AI Chip Costs:** Apple announced plans to increase product prices, with CEO Tim Cook citing "unsustainable" surges in memory chip costs driven by the AI boom.
    *   Memory chip prices, like RAM, have reportedly more than doubled since October 2025.
    *   Global smartphone prices are projected to rise by approximately 20% in 2026.
    *   US President Trump confirmed Apple's agreement to partner with Intel for US-based chip design and manufacturing.
    *   [Sources: https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c3wyxvqdx1zo, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/intel-gains-10-after-trump-says-company-will-partner-with-apple-on-us-chip-design.html, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/wall-street-recovers-from-fed-slump-and-the-next-step-for-amazons-ai-chip-ambitions.html]
*   **Indian IT Stocks Decline After Accenture Warning:** Indian IT stocks, including Tata Consultancy Services and Infosys, fell by up to 7% after Accenture lowered its FY26 revenue guidance.
    *   Accenture cited the Middle East conflict and broader macro uncertainty as contributing factors.
    *   Analysts express caution for the Indian IT sector due to AI disruption and increased competition.
    *   [Source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/india-it-stocks-fall-accenture-cuts-revenue-forecast.html]
*   **Japan's Yen Remains Weak:** Despite a $73 billion foreign exchange intervention and a Bank of Japan rate hike (highest since 1995), the Japanese yen continues to hover near a 40-year low against the US dollar (past 161).
    *   The persistent weakness is attributed to the wide US-Japan interest rate differential, Japan's reliance on energy imports, and Prime Minister Takaichi's reflationary policies.
    *   Japan's core inflation held steady at 1.4% in May, but producer prices rose 6.3% due to energy costs.
    *   [Sources: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/japan-yen-intervention-boj-rate-hike.html, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/japan-yen-161-dollar-intervention-risk-currency-markets.html, https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/19/japan-core-inflation-holds-steady-in-may-matching-expectations-despite-energy-price-concerns.html]

    *   This trend is supported by Beijing's efforts to internationalize its currency and ease capital restrictions.
