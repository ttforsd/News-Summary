## Executive summary

*   **US-Iran ceasefire collapse:** President Trump declared the ceasefire with Iran "over" after fresh US strikes in retaliation for attacks on tankers in the Strait of Hormuz; Iran responded by targeting US military sites.
*   **NATO Summit tensions:** President Trump criticized several NATO allies, particularly Spain, for insufficient defense spending, and reignited discussions about US control of Greenland.

## International Relations & Diplomacy

*   **US-Iran Ceasefire Declared Over Amid Escalating Strikes:**
    *   President Trump stated the ceasefire agreement with Iran is "over" following overnight exchanges of fire.
    *   The US launched "powerful strikes" on Iranian IRGC boats and other targets after attacks on three tankers in the Strait of Hormuz.
    *   Iran's Foreign Minister condemned the US actions as a violation of the interim deal and retaliated by targeting US military sites in Bahrain and Kuwait.

*   **NATO Summit Focuses on Defense and Internal Divisions:**
    *   President Trump criticized Spain for inadequate defense spending, stating he wants "nothing to do with Spain" and suggested cutting trade.
    *   Trump reiterated his desire for US control over Greenland, a Danish territory, linking it to global protection.

    *   This aims to bolster Ukraine's defense against Russia's ballistic missile attacks, which have intensified.
